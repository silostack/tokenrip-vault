# Your Next Big Deal Already Pulled a Permit

## Core argument

Companies telegraph capital-expenditure intent months before issuing an RFP — through building permits, expansion announcements, regulatory filings, hiring patterns, and equipment-specific approvals. Financiers who detect these signals early can shape the deal structure; those who wait for the RFP compete on price alone. AI's role is scale, not discovery: "AI changes the economics of monitoring, not the existence of the signal."

## Key points

- The signal chain (permits → expansion news → regulatory filings → hiring → equipment approvals) exists *before* any financing decision is made — the earlier a financier sees it, the more structuring leverage they have.
- **Caveat, load-bearing:** the article gives no concrete examples — no specific agencies, permit types, or quantified lead-time stats. Most of the supporting detail is paywalled. It's directionally correct but doesn't itself constitute evidence beyond assertion.
- The piece's own framing puts the emphasis on **workflow discipline** (converting signals into prioritized outreach) over the technology — echoing that the hard part is operationalizing the monitoring, not just having access to the data.

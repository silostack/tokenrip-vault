# When AI Makes Things Up — Hallucination Risk for EF Leaders

## Core argument

The danger of AI in equipment finance isn't obvious errors — it's "confident fabrications that slip quietly into strategy discussions, forecasts and board-level materials." AI produces what *looks* right, not what it knows to be true, and EF-specific surfaces are especially exposed because outputs are hard to sanity-check against ground truth in the moment.

## Key points

- Named vulnerability points specific to EF:
  - **Credit underwriting** — AI may cite credible-sounding but outdated growth trends.
  - **Asset valuation** — fabricated or misclassified market comparables.
  - **Residual forecasting** — missing structural shifts (emissions regs, fleet cycles) that AI has no structural reason to catch.
  - **Portfolio analytics** — plausible but unsupported correlations "explaining" performance changes.
- Five recommended governance guardrails: (1) AI for drafting/synthesis only, never final factual authority; (2) scale human-review intensity to financial/reputational risk; (3) never base a financial decision solely on AI output; (4) independently verify every statistic/citation/claim; (5) human accountability persists regardless of AI's role.
- Domain expertise remains irreplaceable for judgment calls AI structurally can't make — recognizing an early market inflection vs. a temporary anomaly.
- The article's stance: the answer is human judgment layered on AI, not avoidance of AI.

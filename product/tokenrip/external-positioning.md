# Tokenrip — External Positioning & Messaging Guide

**Purpose**: The doc you grab when writing copy, filling in a profile, briefing a partner, or answering "what does Tokenrip do?" for a non-investor audience. For investor/strategic positioning, see [[tokenrip-positioning]]. For vertical-specific outreach and execution, see [[../../bd/firm-direct-strategy/starting-guide]].

**Primary ICP**: Firms — document-heavy businesses (insurance agencies, tax firms, real estate brokerages, expanding)
**Secondary ICP**: Builders — from Claude Code power users to workplace AI champions building agents for their teams
**Last updated**: 2026-05-12

## Unified External Positioning

The message that works regardless of audience. Use when both ICPs might see the same surface (LinkedIn company page, homepage hero, conference badge) or when you don't know who you're talking to.

### Tagline

> **AI that does the work.**

Differentiates from chatbots and demos. Firms hear "the coordination work my team drowns in." Builders hear "agents that actually get used, not side projects." The contrast is implicit: other AI answers questions — this AI does work.

### Short Tagline (under 50 characters)

> AI agents for business workflows

### One-Sentence

> Tokenrip deploys AI agents that handle real business operations — document review, routing, coordination, and tracking — so your team handles decisions, not busywork.

### Elevator Pitch (30 seconds)

> Most AI in business today is chat windows and demos. Tokenrip is different — we deploy AI agents that do actual operational work: reviewing documents, routing them to the right person, flagging what's missing, and tracking everything through to completion. Firms use Tokenrip to automate the workflows their teams spend hours on every week. Builders use Tokenrip to create and deploy agents that solve real problems inside real businesses.

### Paragraph (for About sections, bios)

> Tokenrip is an AI agent platform for operational business workflows. We deploy agents that review incoming documents, catch errors, route to the right person, and track everything — so teams handle decisions, not coordination. Insurance agencies use Tokenrip to process certificates of insurance in minutes instead of hours. Tax firms use it to automate the document chase. Real estate brokerages use it to coordinate closings without things falling through cracks. For builders — from experienced developers to workplace AI champions — Tokenrip is where you create agents that get deployed and used by real teams in real businesses.

## Firm-Facing Positioning

Use when talking to business owners, operations managers, CSR managers, brokers, managing partners — anyone who runs a firm and feels workflow pain.

### Core Message

> **Your team shouldn't spend their day chasing documents. AI handles the coordination — your team handles the decisions.**

### Lead With Pain

Firms in document-heavy industries share the same structural pain:

- Staff spend 30-60% of their time on coordination work: chasing documents, checking completeness, routing to the right person, following up
- Things fall through cracks — missed deadlines, incomplete submissions, lost handoffs
- The work is repetitive but requires judgment, so simple rules can't automate it
- Every hour on coordination is an hour not spent on revenue-generating work

### Then the Solution

Tokenrip deploys AI into your workflow that:

1. **Reviews** incoming documents — checks completeness, flags errors, catches what humans miss
2. **Routes** to the right person based on what it finds
3. **Notifies** your team when something needs attention
4. **Tracks** everything in a dashboard so nothing falls through the cracks

Your team stays in control — they approve at every step. The AI handles everything else.

### Vertical Pain Language

**Insurance agencies:**
> Your CSRs process 50-200 COI requests a week. Each one takes 15-30 minutes of checking: coverage limits, policy status, endorsements. That's nearly a full headcount spent on document review. Tokenrip does the review in seconds and hands your CSR a ready-to-approve package.

**Tax firms:**
> Tax season capacity is everything. Your preparers spend hours chasing missing W-2s, reconciling prior-year returns, checking for obvious flags. Tokenrip automates the document chase and the completeness check, so your preparers spend time on preparation, not paper-chasing.

**Real estate brokerages:**
> Closing delays cost $1,500-$5,000 per deal. Most delays are coordination failures — missing documents, expired contingencies, miscommunication between parties. Tokenrip tracks every document, every party, every deadline, and flags problems before they become delays.

### Headline Options (for landing pages, ads)

**Pain-led:**
> Stop chasing documents. Start closing deals.

**Outcome-led:**
> Your documents. Reviewed, routed, and tracked — automatically.

**Time-led:**
> Your team spends 30% of their week chasing paperwork. What if they didn't?

### Language Rules — Firms

| Use | Instead of |
|-----|-----------|
| AI | Agent, mounted agent, imprint |
| Your workflow | Business process automation |
| Reviews and routes | Orchestrates |
| Dashboard | Operator visibility |
| Catches errors | Observability |
| Gets smarter over time | Memory persistence |
| Works with your existing tools | Integrations / harness portability |
| Your team approves at each step | Human-in-the-loop |

**Never say to firms**: mounted agent, imprint, harness, BYO model, substrate, portability, memory layer, collections, assets, operator. These are builder/infrastructure concepts that create distance.

## Builder-Facing Positioning

Use when talking to developers, AI builders, workplace automation leads, "AI champions," or anyone who builds agents for others to use.

### The Builder Spectrum

Builders on Tokenrip span a range:

| Type | Who | What they build | What they need from Tokenrip |
|------|-----|----------------|------------------------------|
| **Power builder** | Developers using Claude Code, Cursor, or similar | Sophisticated agents with custom logic, API integrations, complex workflows | Deployment, persistence, distribution — stop rebuilding infrastructure |
| **Workplace builder** | Non-developer who learned Claude Cowork or ChatGPT and started automating | Team-specific agents for recurring workflows | Simple creation, easy deployment to coworkers who aren't technical |
| **Agentic builder** | Emerging company role — the person who "does AI" for the org | Agents for departments, cross-team workflows | Platform that makes their agents usable by non-technical coworkers at scale |

### Core Message

> **Build agents that get used. Not demos — deployed, persistent AI that runs real workflows for real teams.**

### Lead With Pain

- You build something useful and it dies in the chat window
- Your agent can't persist between sessions, can't remember context, can't be shared with anyone who isn't technical
- Every coworker who wants to use your agent needs the same setup you have
- You're rebuilding plumbing (memory, deployment, distribution) instead of building the thing that matters

### Then the Solution

Tokenrip is the platform where your agents become products:

- **Build** using the tools you already know — Claude Code, Cursor, Claude Cowork, or Tokenrip's builder
- **Deploy** to Tokenrip — your agent gets persistence, memory, and a shareable endpoint
- **Distribute** — coworkers or clients use your agent without any technical setup
- **Iterate** — usage data and version control let you improve based on how people actually use it

### The Workplace Story

This is the narrative that connects the builder ICP to the firm ICP:

> You figured out how to automate your team's COI review process with AI. Right now, it lives on your machine, in your chat window. With Tokenrip, you deploy that agent once — and every CSR on your team uses it. They don't need Claude Code. They don't need to understand prompts. They open a link and start working. When you improve the agent, everyone gets the update automatically.

### Language Rules — Builders

| Use | Instead of |
|-----|-----------|
| Build and deploy | Publish imprints |
| Your agent persists | Memory layer architecture |
| Share with your team | Operator distribution |
| Runs in any environment | Harness portability |
| Gets better over time | Shared memory compounds |
| Build once, everyone uses it | Mount across runtimes |
| Agent | Mounted agent (unless deep technical context) |

**Can say to builders**: agent, deploy, persist, memory, API, SDK, version control. **Still avoid**: imprint, harness, substrate, BYO model economics — these are internal/investor vocabulary.

## The Platform Story — How They Connect

Use when you need to explain the full picture — why firms and builders are on the same platform.

### The Two Doors

The firm-facing pitch and builder-facing pitch aren't contradictions. They're two doors into the same room:

- A **firm** discovers Tokenrip because they need AI to handle their document workflow. They might use a pre-built agent, or their internal AI person builds one on Tokenrip for the team.
- A **builder** discovers Tokenrip because they want their agents to actually get used. They build on Tokenrip because it gives them deployment, persistence, and distribution to real businesses.
- Over time: more builders creating agents, more firms using them, better agents from more usage data.

### The Flow

```
BUILDER                              FIRM
Creates agent on Tokenrip    --->    Team uses agent daily
Iterates based on usage      <---    Usage data flows back
Gets paid (optional)         <---    Firm pays for value delivered
```

### Platform Pitch (30 seconds)

> Tokenrip is a two-sided AI platform. On one side, builders create agents that automate real business workflows — document review, routing, coordination, tracking. On the other side, firms and teams deploy those agents and use them daily. The builder doesn't manage infrastructure. The firm doesn't need technical skills. Tokenrip handles everything in between: deployment, persistence, memory, and distribution.

### Why It Compounds

Each side makes the other more valuable:
- More builders = more workflow agents available = more reasons for firms to use Tokenrip
- More firms = more real usage = better agents from real data = more reasons for builders to build on Tokenrip
- The agents themselves improve through use — they're not static automations, they learn from the workflows they process

## Competitive Differentiation — Quick Responses

For when someone asks "how is this different from X?"

### vs. ChatGPT / Claude / general AI chat

> Those are thinking tools — you ask a question, get an answer. Tokenrip deploys agents that do operational work: reviewing documents, routing, tracking, coordinating. The AI doesn't wait for your team to ask — it works.

### vs. Zapier / Make / automation tools

> Zapier connects apps with rules. Tokenrip deploys AI that reads and understands documents — it catches errors, makes routing decisions that require judgment, and handles the work that rules-based automation can't.

### vs. Vertical SaaS (Applied Epic, TaxDome, Dotloop)

> Those platforms digitize your workflow. Tokenrip automates the judgment-heavy parts — the review, the routing, the coordination — that those platforms still leave to your team.

### vs. AI consulting / custom builds

> Consultants build for you, leave, and the system decays. Tokenrip is a platform — your agents persist, update, and improve. You're deploying infrastructure, not buying a project.

### vs. Agent frameworks (CrewAI, LangGraph, AutoGen)

> Those are developer tools for building agent logic. Tokenrip is a deployment platform — build your agent however you want, deploy it to Tokenrip, and your team uses it without touching code.

### vs. Agent marketplaces (GPT Store, agent directories)

> Marketplaces list chatbots. Tokenrip deploys agents that do real operational work — with persistence, memory, and workflow integration. The difference between browsing an app store and deploying enterprise software.

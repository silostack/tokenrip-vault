# Weekly KPI Scorecard — Simon + Alek

*Created 2026-05-18. Owner: shared. Update the scorecard section every week.*

This file is the shared KPI framework and running scorecard for Tokenrip's two founders. It is self-contained — if you're reading this cold, everything you need is here.

---

## Context — Why This Exists

Tokenrip is a deployed product (the mounted-agent substrate) with **$0 revenue**. Both founders are in full-swing outreach. The current direction (as of 2026-05-18) is **active-demand interception**: pursuing buyers who have *already posted and funded* AI-agent work — Upwork job posts and companies hiring for AI-agent roles — rather than cold-outreaching firms who may or may not have pain. The thesis: find painkiller-grade pain the substrate can serve, take engagements, build the platform through them (the consulting-as-platform playbook — AWS, Palantir).

Two founders running outreach in parallel need **one shared definition of progress** so they can align weekly, see who's converting, and find the bottleneck together. Without it, two people optimize two different things and neither knows if the company is moving.

This framework follows YC's startup-metrics guidance (`__RESOURCES/yc-startup-metrics-frameworks.md`): one primary KPI, 3–5 secondary, weekly goals laddering to a longer-term goal, agreed definitions, top-down + bottom-up targets, and — above all — honesty.

It is deliberately **low-ceremony**. Five lines per founder, 15 minutes twice a week. Run it for two weeks, then iterate the template. Do not perfect it now.

---

## 1. The Primary KPI (shared, company-level)

> **Primary: Qualified conversations completed per week.**
> The moment real revenue exists, revenue *replaces* this as the primary.

Both founders ladder up to the *same* number. We are not competing on different metrics — we are two inputs to one funnel. Longer-term goal this ladders to: **first paid engagement**.

### Agreed definition — freeze this

A **qualified conversation** = *a live call with someone who has budget authority or real influence over an AI-agent decision.*

- Agree on this wording together before the first report. Tighten it if needed — then freeze it.
- **Do not redefine it later because a number looks bad.** If conversations are low, fix the bottleneck — don't move the goalposts.

---

## 2. The Weekly Scorecard — same 5 lines, both founders

Each founder fills in the same five lines every week. Lines 1, 3, 4, 5 are identical and directly comparable. Line 2 is channel-specific (Simon: Upwork submissions + job-req outreaches; Alek: his channel's outreach volume).

```
FOUNDER: ___        WEEK OF: ___

1. PRIMARY    — Qualified conversations completed:   target ___ / actual ___
2. INPUT      — Outreach sent (your channel):        target ___ / actual ___
3. CONVERSION — Outreach → conversation rate:        ___%
4. QUALITY    — Of conversations, how many deal-shaped / architecture-grade?  ___
5. BOTTLENECK — The one thing most blocking line 1 next week: ___
```

**Line 4 note:** "architecture-grade" = the buyer's problem genuinely needs what the substrate uniquely provides (memory, persistence, portability). This is read in aggregate over time — across ~15 conversations, what fraction are architecture-grade? That ratio tells us whether the mounted-agent wedge lives in this population.

---

## 3. The Ritual — 15 minutes, twice a week

**Pre-week (founder sync):** Each founder sets targets for lines 1 and 2. Set them both ways:
- *Top-down:* "We want a first paid engagement in N weeks → that needs X conversations/week."
- *Bottom-up:* "Realistically, given my channel, I can do Y next week."
- Land the target between the two.

**End-week (review):** Fill in actuals. Read lines 3, 4, 5 aloud. Did targets hold? Ask *why* — three times if line 1 didn't move; the real reason is rarely the obvious one.

---

## 4. Discipline Rules (the four that matter)

1. **Don't hide zeros.** If conversations = 0, write 0. Honesty drives the fix.
2. **One primary — don't optimize two hard things at once.** Conversations now. Not conversations *and* revenue *and* substrate density simultaneously.
3. **When the primary doesn't move, ask "why" three times.** The bottleneck is usually not the first answer.
4. **Don't change definitions when numbers look bad.** Fix the bottleneck instead.

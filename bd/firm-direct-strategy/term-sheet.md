# Design Partner Term Sheet — Starting Point

**Status**: Draft v0.1 — iterate from here
**Purpose**: One-page commercial framework to bring into every prospect conversation. Locks down the deal before we get pulled into custom negotiation in real time.
**Audience**: Tokenrip's first vertical pilot prospect ("design partner")

---

## The Headline Offer

> Tokenrip will build a working AI workflow stack for your business — running on Tokenrip's infrastructure, customized to your domain — in **6 weeks**, for **$25,000**, with **50% off list price** in exchange for design partner status.

This is the elevator version. The rest is structure.

---

## Deal Structure

### 1. Commercials

| Element | Terms | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Design partner fee** | $25,000 USD (one-time) | List price anchored at $50K; 50% off for design partner status |
| **Payment schedule** | 50% on signature ($12.5K), 50% on go-live ($12.5K) | Go-live = first end-to-end workflow run in production |
| **Engagement length** | 6 weeks build + 4 weeks support = **10 weeks total** | Build + iterate + transfer to steady-state |
| **Post-pilot pricing** | $2,500–$5,000/month commercial license, depending on usage tier | Optional. Either party can decline to continue. |

**Why $25K**: above the "no-risk experiment" threshold (forces real internal sponsorship), below most procurement tripwires (~$50K), realistic for two-founder bandwidth at a 6-week build.

### 2. Scope (the cap)

- **Locked at kickoff** via a one-page workflow scope doc (signed by both sides at week zero)
- **Includes**:
  - 1 primary workflow (e.g. closing package routing for real estate)
  - Up to 3 integrations from a standard list (Slack, Google Docs/Drive, Microsoft 365, Gmail, DocuSign, native portal)
  - Mounted agent for review/routing/notification
  - Operator dashboard
  - Up to 5 user seats during pilot
- **Out-of-scope** work documented and quoted separately
- **Maximum 2 in-scope changes** during build phase; further changes triggered as out-of-scope

**Why this matters**: scope cap is the line between "design partnership" and "consulting trap." Without this, every customer ask becomes a yes, and you're rebuilding their internal tools forever.

### 3. Exclusivity

- **Vertical exclusivity** for **90 days** post-go-live
- **Defined by**: up to 5 named direct competitors (provided by customer at signature)
- **Geographic scope**: none (we don't lock by region)
- **Renewable**: by mutual agreement at end of pilot, with revised commercials

**Why limited**: 90 days is enough for them to feel like they have an edge; short enough that we can move on Vertical #2 without waiting them out. Direct-competitor list keeps it specific (not "all of real estate" — only the 5 firms they actually compete with).

### 4. Reference Rights

- **Logo on tokenrip.com** homepage and case studies
- **Written case study** (1-2 pages, subject to PR approval — final copy goes to customer for signoff)
- **2 reference calls per quarter** for 12 months (we provide the prospect, they spend ~30 min on a call)
- **Optional**: joint conference appearance, podcast episode, or co-written piece

**Why this matters**: the next 5 design partners will be much easier to close with this customer's logo and a real case study. Reference rights are the most undervalued element in early-stage deals — make sure they're contractually granted, not vibe-based.

### 5. IP / Ownership

| Asset | Owner | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Tokenrip platform code** | Tokenrip | Core platform, never transferred |
| **Application templates / patterns** | Tokenrip | Reusable across other customers; this clause is non-negotiable |
| **Domain-specific custom logic** | Tokenrip (with customer license) | Logic specific to their workflow lives in templates Tokenrip can refine and reuse anonymously; customer gets perpetual usage license |
| **Customer data** | Customer | Their data is theirs. We don't claim it. |
| **Workflow definitions** | Customer | Their proprietary process docs, SOPs, etc. |
| **Imprint config** | Tokenrip retains ability to anonymize patterns into shared knowledge layer | Customer can opt out of contribution |

**The key clause** (must be in every contract):
> *"Tokenrip retains ownership of all platform code, integration patterns, and generalized workflow templates produced during the engagement. Customer receives a perpetual, non-exclusive license to use the deployed application. Customer-specific workflow definitions and data remain the exclusive property of Customer."*

This single sentence is the difference between a productized vertical motion and a custom services consultancy.

### 6. Termination / Off-ramp

- **Notice**: 30 days either way after pilot complete
- **On termination**: Customer keeps deployed app and continues using under license; Tokenrip retains all generalized patterns and templates
- **Refunds**: any unspent prepaid amount returned within 14 days
- **Data export**: Tokenrip provides full data export within 30 days of termination

## What We Ask For (in addition to cash)

These are the non-cash terms that often matter more than the dollar amount:

1. **Named executive sponsor** at the customer (typically COO, VP Ops, or owner) — meets weekly for 30 min during build
2. **Workflow access** — at least 2 hours of process interview time in week 1 with the people who actually do the work
3. **Production usage commitment** — they actually use the deployed app on real workflows during the pilot, not just demo
4. **Feedback rhythm** — Friday async write-ups during build, weekly sync after go-live
5. **Reference behavior** — willingness to take a reference call within 30 days of go-live, even if outcome is mixed

**Without these, the engagement is theater.** The customer paying real money but not committing real time = no signal, no learning, no case study. Walk if any of these aren't agreed.

## What's Negotiable vs. Non-Negotiable

| Element | Position |
|---|---|
| Cash amount | **Negotiable** — can flex 15K–40K based on customer size and depth of engagement |
| Payment schedule | **Negotiable** — could go all-on-go-live for trust-building |
| Engagement length | **Slightly negotiable** — 4-12 week range |
| Vertical exclusivity period | **Negotiable** — 30-180 days |
| Reference rights | **Hold the line** — non-negotiable |
| IP / pattern reuse | **Hold the line** — non-negotiable |
| Scope cap mechanism | **Hold the line** — non-negotiable |
| Named exec sponsor | **Hold the line** — non-negotiable |

The non-negotiables are the things that determine whether this becomes a productized motion or a one-off consulting gig. Every concession on these makes vertical #2 harder.

## How to Use This in a Conversation

1. **Don't lead with terms.** Lead with the demo and the discovery. Terms come out in conversation 2 or 3, when they say "this is interesting, what would it look like?"
2. **Hand them a one-pager** that summarizes the headline offer + commercials. Detailed terms come at contract stage.
3. **The number is $25K, not "around 25"**. Be precise. Vagueness reads as flexibility.
4. **Position as a bargain**: "Design partner pricing is half off list. After this cohort closes, we go to $50K/engagement."
5. **Emphasize speed**: 6 weeks to a working system is the headline. Most enterprise SaaS pilots take longer than that just to start.

## Open questions to resolve before first pitch

- [ ] **Legal**: do we need a basic MSA + SOW template? Probably yes. Pull a clean template, customize.
- [ ] **Contract counsel**: who reviews? Use template + cheap counsel review, not full custom drafting.
- [ ] **Tax / billing entity**: which entity bills the customer? Tokenrip Inc., RebelFi, or other?
- [ ] **Banking**: where does the $25K go? Stripe Atlas, Mercury, etc. — already set up?
- [ ] **Sales tax / W-9**: customer will ask for these. Have them ready.

These are not strategic questions, but they will block a deal from closing if not pre-resolved. Worth one afternoon to handle.

## The Math

5 booked calls/week is the goal. Working backwards:

| Stage | Conversion | Volume needed |
|---|---|---|
| **Booked calls** | (target) | 5 / week |
| **Replies → call-yes** | ~50% | 10 replies / week |
| **Cold messages → reply** | 5–10% | 100–200 messages / week |

So: **roughly 30 outbound contacts per day, every weekday.** Or fewer if Alek's content drives inbound (which we expect to lift over time).

This is sustainable for one person if it's the priority and the tooling is right. It is not sustainable as a side effort.

## Outbound Strategy — LinkedIn

### Targeting

For real estate (default vertical):

**ICP titles**:
- Broker / Owner (small-mid brokerages)
- Managing Broker
- Director of Operations
- VP Sales / VP Operations
- COO (mid-size brokerages, RE tech)
- Founder / CEO (RE tech, proptech)

**ICP firmographics**:
- 10–100 agents (above sole-proprietor, below national-franchise scale)
- Tech-aware: has a website beyond IDX, has signed something into Cloze/Dotloop/Skyslope
- $5M–$100M annual transaction volume range

**Where to find them**:
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator (if budget allows; otherwise free LinkedIn search)
- BrokerCheck / state real estate licensing rolls
- Inman News writer mentions, Inman Connect attendee lists
- Real estate tech podcasts (guest lists)
- T3 Sixty top brokerage rankings

### List Building Workflow

1. **Daily**: Alek pulls 30 prospects matching ICP
2. **Per prospect**: Note their firm, recent post/announcement, any AI-relevant context
3. **Tag**: vertical, persona, source, status
4. **Track in CRM** (Attio, since you already use it) — pipeline stages: Sourced → Touched → Replied → Call Booked → Call Done → Demo Done → Pilot Discussion → Closed/Lost

### Outreach Sequence

A 3-touch cadence, all on LinkedIn. **No mass-blast tools.** Personalized, founder-led, low-volume-high-quality.

**Touch 1 — Connection request + opener (Day 0)**

Hyper-short. Reference one specific thing about them or their firm. State a compelling premise. No pitch.

> *Example*: "Hi [Name] — I run a small AI startup focused on coordination workflows in real estate. I noticed [Firm] has been growing fast in [region/segment]. Curious how you handle the coordination overhead in your closings — happy to share what I'm seeing across the market if useful."

**Touch 2 — Soft value drop (Day 4)**

If accepted, no reply. Drop one piece of value. Could be:
- A relevant statistic
- A quick observation about their firm/market
- A 90-second loom showing the demo

> *Example*: "Following up — sharing this 90-sec walkthrough of an AI agent handling a closing package. Recorded specifically with brokerages in mind: [link]. No pitch, just curious if this resonates with the kind of friction you see."

**Touch 3 — Direct ask (Day 9)**

Direct, specific call-to-action. Calendly link.

> *Example*: "Last note — would 20 minutes next week be useful? I'd love to compare notes on the AI workflow gap in real estate, no pitch. Booking link if it's a fit: [link]. If not, no worries — happy to stay in touch."

### Conversion expectations

- **Connection acceptance**: 30–50% (good targeting + good profile)
- **Reply rate over 3 touches**: 5–15% (this is the volume number to hit)
- **Reply → call booked**: 30–50%
- **Calls booked from 100 prospects**: 2–5

### Required tooling

- **CRM**: Attio (already in use). Set up a "BD pipeline" view.
- **Calendar**: Calendly link with vertical-aware booking page.
- **Tracker spreadsheet** (or Attio fields): outreach status, response, follow-up date.
- **Demo recording**: 2–3 min screencast (created post-build).
- **One-pager term sheet**: PDF version of the headline offer (from `term-sheet.md`).

### Founder-led, not BDR-style

This is **founder outreach**, not SDR-blast. Don't sound like a recruiter or a sales tool. Sound like a real human with a specific point of view who's curious about the recipient's world. Quality over volume — if 100 messages/week feels formulaic, do 50/week with twice the personalization.

| Geography | Start regional, expand later |
| Keywords | "independent agency" OR "P&C" |

### The COI Angle

Supply-side COI automation has **zero competitors**. Every COI player (myCOI, PINS, Certus) tracks COIs that companies *receive* from vendors. Nobody helps agencies *process and issue* them. This is the lead hook.

CSRs spend 60-80% of their time on repetitive operational tasks. COI requests alone consume 50-200 requests/week × 15-30 min each = a full headcount of capacity.

ROI justification: one CSR costs $45-65K loaded. Automating 50% of COI processing pays for the product immediately.

### Outreach Sequence (Insurance-Specific)

**Touch 1 — Connection request (Day 0)**

> Hi [Name] — I'm building AI tools for independent agencies. I noticed [Agency] handles commercial P&C in [region]. Quick question: how many COI requests does your team process per week? We're working on something that automates the review step entirely. Happy to share what we're seeing if useful.

**Touch 2 — Value drop (Day 4-5)**

> Following up — put together a 90-sec walkthrough of AI handling a COI request: checks coverage requirements, flags gaps, routes to CSR for approval. [link] No pitch — just curious if this matches the friction you see on your side.

**Touch 3 — Direct ask (Day 9-10)**

> Last note — would 20 minutes be useful? I'm talking to a handful of agency owners about how AI could take the repetitive ops off their CSRs' plates. Not selling anything yet — genuinely gathering intel. [Calendly link] if it's a fit.

### Volume (Calibrated for Simon's Split Time)

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Messages/day | 15-20 |
| Messages/week | 75-100 |
| Connection acceptance | 30-50% |
| Reply rate (3 touches) | 5-15% |
| Reply → call booked | 30-50% |
| Expected calls/week | 1-5 |

### Sales Navigator Tips (First-Timer)

1. Don't InMail — connection request + note always outperforms
2. Engage on their posts before you message (like, comment, be visible)
3. Monday and Tuesday mornings get the best acceptance rates
4. Personalize the first line every time
5. Start with a tracking spreadsheet, migrate to Attio CRM at >50 contacts
6. Set up 2-3 saved searches, check weekly for new matches
7. Use boolean search: quotes for exact phrases, OR for alternatives

## Inbound Strategy — Alek's Content Gameplan

### The premise

Alek already has a LinkedIn following and content velocity. Pivot it from generalist commentary to **AI workflows in document-heavy industries.** Build authority in the niche, generate inbound, give cold outreach more credibility ("the guy posting about AI workflows in real estate" is much warmer than "some guy DMing me").

Expected timeline:
- **Weeks 1–2**: rebuild content rhythm with vertical focus. No inbound yet.
- **Weeks 3–4**: posts compound, comment activity grows. Light inbound starts.
- **Weeks 5–8**: reliable inbound. Cold outreach reply rate lifts because prospects recognize the name.

### Content pillars (4 themes, rotated)

**Pillar 1 — The pain (most important)**
*Posts that articulate the pain we're solving in concrete, specific terms.*

- "How a single missed checkbox costs a real estate brokerage $5K"
- "The 'who has the file?' question — how much it costs the average closing"
- "Why coordination, not transactions, is the real bottleneck in [vertical]"

**Pillar 2 — Why the incumbents can't fix this**
*Posts that establish positioning advantage — incumbents will fail at AI workflows.*

- "Why AI features bolted onto your existing software won't work"
- "The Microsoft / Google paradox: they can't build the layer their customers actually need"
- "The infrastructure tax on every AI tool you use today"

**Pillar 3 — The new shape**
*Posts that paint the world we're building toward, anchored by Tokenrip's mounted-agent thesis.*

- "What it looks like when documents move themselves"
- "The mounted agent: a different way to think about AI in your business"
- "Why your agent will outlive the tool you bought it from"

**Pillar 4 — Stories from the field**
*Posts that drop concrete examples, observations, customer quotes (anonymized at first, named later).*

- "Spent the morning shadowing a brokerage closing coordinator. Here's what I learned."
- "Three operators told me the same thing this week."
- "A pattern across the firms I've talked to."

### Cadence

- **3–5 LinkedIn posts per week** — mix of pillars
- **1 longer thought-piece per week** — could go on Substack, Tokenrip blog, or LinkedIn article. Repurposed from the best-performing short post.
- **15–30 min/day engaging on others' posts** — comment thoughtfully on prospects' posts and on relevant industry leaders. This is where new connections come from organically.

### Format mix

- **Text posts**: highest distribution on LinkedIn currently
- **Carousels**: every 2 weeks, for the most insight-dense topics
- **Short videos / Loom embeds**: occasional, when a demo or a tour adds value beyond text
- **Polls**: rare but useful for engagement spikes

### Calls to action

Every post ends with one of:
- "DM me if you want to compare notes"
- "Booking 5 conversations with [persona] this week — link in profile"
- "Working on a [demo / report / write-up] — comment 'send' and I'll share"

Never push hard. Always invite.

### Profile setup

Alek's LinkedIn profile is the landing page. It needs:
- **Headline**: Clear positioning. e.g., "Building AI workflow tools for [vertical] | Co-founder, Tokenrip"
- **About**: 200-word version of the strategic thesis. Pain → why incumbents fail → what we're building → invitation
- **Featured section**: Demo video, term sheet PDF, top blog post
- **Booking link** in About / Featured

## Coordination Between Outbound and Content

The two motions feed each other:

1. **Content provides ammunition for outbound**. Reference recent posts in DMs: "Saw your reaction to my post on [X] — would love to dig in."
2. **Outbound provides material for content**. Anonymized observations from prospect calls become the next pillar 4 post.
3. **Calls go into Attio**. Patterns from the calls become next month's content themes.

**Weekly rhythm**:
- Monday: Alek pulls week's prospect list, drafts week's content posts
- Tuesday–Thursday: outbound + posts shipped daily
- Friday: review week's data — replies, calls booked, content engagement. Adjust next week.

## What "Working" Looks Like

Set checkpoints. Measure honestly. Course-correct fast.

| Week | Metric | What it tells us |
|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | Profile + content rhythm live; 100 prospects sourced | Setup is real |
| Week 2 | First 3 calls booked from cold outreach | Outbound math works |
| Week 3 | First inbound reply from content | Content is starting |
| Week 4 | 5 calls/week sustained | Motion is real |
| Week 6 | First demo conversation that turns into "send me terms" | Demo + offer working |
| Week 8 | First signed pilot OR clear "this vertical is wrong" signal | Learning is real |

**Failure modes to watch for**:
- Lots of connections, no replies → message quality / ICP problem
- Replies but no calls booked → CTA / Calendly friction
- Calls but no demos → discovery script weak
- Demos but no pilot interest → product / positioning problem
- All metrics OK but no signed deal in 8 weeks → offer / closing motion problem

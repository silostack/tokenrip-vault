## The Question Behind the KPI

Simon proposed: *"# booked calls per week until we get some sort of revenue."*

That's a reasonable starting point but it has a known failure mode: **you can hit 5 calls/week with 5 useless calls.** Booked-call volume is necessary but not sufficient. The right structure is a primary process KPI for now, with a clear shift to a leading-revenue indicator once outreach is producing reliable volume, and a North Star that keeps us honest about why we're doing any of this.

This is exactly the case Divya named in the KPI talk: *"an early enterprise business with a long sales cycle might choose letters of intent."* For us, the long-sales-cycle leading indicator is **active term sheet conversations** — a step closer to revenue than booked calls, a step before signed contracts.

| Metric | Value |

| Deadline | Sun July 5 (end of Week 8 of outreach) |

## Primary Process KPI (Now Through Week 3) — Booked Discovery Calls per Week

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Target ramp | Week 1: 1-2. Week 2: 2-3. Week 3: 3-4. **Week 4+: 5/week sustained.** |
| Definition | A 30-min call with an ICP-matched prospect, on the calendar with a Calendly confirmation. Not "we should chat sometime." Not a vague reply. **Confirmed booking.** |
| Source attribution | Tag each as: cold outbound, content-inbound, warm intro, or referral. We need to know which channel is producing. |
| Measurement cadence | Weekly check-in every Sunday. Logged in Attio. Reviewed against ICP fit (was the call actually with the right person?). |

**Why this is primary now (not revenue)**: Our funnel is empty. Until the funnel has volume, optimizing the bottom of the funnel is theatrical — there's nothing to convert. Booked calls is the earliest reliable signal that the top of the funnel is real.

**Why 5/week is the target**: that's the rate that produces ~20 calls/month, which is the volume needed to expect 1-2 demos from a cold motion, which is the volume needed to expect 1 deal close in 8 weeks. The math comes from `lead-gen-strategy.md`.

**Why this is a process KPI, not a revenue KPI**: this is exactly what Divya warned about — *"if you've launched, your primary KPI should be revenue growth. A non-revenue KPI is rarely the right one."* We use a process KPI here because we're pre-revenue with a long sales cycle, but we **do not** stay on this metric indefinitely. The shift happens at Week 4.

## Leading Indicator (Week 4 → Close) — Active Term Sheet Conversations

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Target | 1 active by Week 6 (June 21). 2+ active by Week 8 (July 5). |
| Definition | A prospect who has seen the demo, met with Simon at least once, and is actively reviewing or negotiating the term sheet. Not "interested." Not "wants to learn more." **Engaging on commercials.** |
| Why this matters | Closest leading indicator to revenue. Booked calls don't pay; signed contracts pay. This metric is the bridge. |

**The shift**: starting Week 4 (June 1), Booked Calls becomes a *secondary* metric and Active Term Sheet Conversations becomes the *primary* indicator. If 5 calls/week is happening but no one is asking for terms, the funnel has volume but no conversion — and that's the problem to solve.

## Secondary KPIs — The Funnel

These keep us honest about whether the primary KPI is moving for the right reasons. **Keep them visible. Update weekly. Diagnose when they drift.**

| Stage                         | Conversion target      | What it tells us                          |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Cold msgs → connection accept | 30-50%                 | ICP targeting + profile credibility       |
| Connection → reply            | 5-15% (over 3 touches) | Message quality + content credibility     |
| Reply → call booked           | 30-50%                 | CTA strength + Calendly friction          |
| Call → demo                   | 50-70%                 | Discovery script + interest qualification |
| Demo → "send terms"           | 30-50%                 | Demo conviction + offer fit               |
|                               |                        |                                           |

**Math at the targets**: 100 cold messages → 35 connections → 7 replies → 3 calls → 2 demos → 0.7 term sheet asks. Roughly. Multiply by weeks-running for cumulative pipeline.

**What "honest" looks like**: if calls/week is at target but reply rate is 30% (way above the 15% ceiling), we're probably counting "any reply" instead of "interested reply." Tighten the definition.

## Vanity Metrics — Things We Will NOT Track as Primary

These are the traps both YC speakers warned about. They feel like progress. They are not.

- ❌ **LinkedIn connections made** — easy to inflate, doesn't predict revenue
- ❌ **Posts published / content engagement** — Alek's content is an *input*, not an outcome. Likes and comments don't pay.
- ❌ **Registry submissions / agent platform listings** — distribution flywheel, not BD outcome
- ❌ **GitHub stars / Tokenrip user count** — wrong product motion entirely
- ❌ **"Conversations had"** vs. "calls booked" — chats in DMs are not the same as 30-min Calendly bookings
- ❌ **GMV-style metrics** — we don't have a GMV equivalent and shouldn't invent one

We can *track* some of these as secondary noise — engagement on Alek's content is useful diagnostic — but they never become a target we're optimizing for.

**Tom's specific warning** that applies to us: *"Page views, unique visitors, GMV, gross transaction value — big numbers that aren't tied to company success."* For us the equivalent is "100 prospects sourced this week!" Sourcing isn't the win. Booking the call with one of them is.

## Burn Rate + Runway

Per Tom's three-core-metrics rule, every B2B founder should know:

- **Net burn rate**: monthly costs minus revenue
- **Runway**: months of cash remaining at current burn

**These are owned by Yoda's accountability layer, not BD.** But the BD motion exists because of them — revenue pressure is the reason we're testing demand fast. If burn rate or runway shifts materially, this gameplan re-prioritizes accordingly.

**Action**: Yoda should confirm Simon has these numbers recent and visible. Not in this doc — in [[agents/yoda/context]] or wherever Yoda tracks runway. Per Tom: *"if revenue, burn, runway aren't at the top of investor updates, the founder is hiding something."* Same applies internally.

## Weekly KPI Review — Sunday Cadence

Every Monday, both Simon and Alek look at:

1. **North Star**: Revenue this week. (Will be $0 until July.)
2. **Primary KPI**: Calls booked this week. Cumulative since outreach started.
3. **Leading indicator**: Term sheet conversations active. (Starts populating Week 4+.)
4. **Funnel rates**: Did each stage hit target conversion this week? Where's the leak?
5. **Channel attribution**: Which channel produced the calls? Cold outbound vs. content-inbound vs. warm intros.
6. **One thing to change**: Based on the data, what's the highest-leverage adjustment for next week? Pick one. Don't change five things.

**Format**: short async write-up shared between Simon and Alek. ~10 minutes to write, ~10 minutes to discuss. Logged so we can look back at week-over-week change.

**Discipline**: don't change KPI definitions when numbers look bad. Tom's rule: *"don't switch from weekly actives to monthly actives just because the number looks better."* If reply rate is 4%, log 4% — don't redefine "reply" to include "viewed but didn't respond."

## Targets — Top-Down Backwards From Goal

Per Divya's framework: set targets both top-down and bottom-up; pick the goal between them.

### Top-down (from $25K signed by July 5)

To sign 1 deal by July 5, we need:
- 1-2 active term sheet conversations by Week 6
- 3-5 demo conversations by Week 5
- 8-12 booked calls in Weeks 4-6
- 5+ calls/week sustained Week 4 onward
- ~150-200 cold contacts/week by Week 4

### Bottom-up (what's realistically achievable from week 1)

Week 1 outreach calibration: ~20 contacts → ~1-2 connections → ~0-1 replies → 0-1 calls. Not bad — that's the soft launch.

Week 2-3: scale to 100/week → ~5 replies → 2-3 calls/week.

Week 4+: sustained 100-200/week + content-inbound starting → 5/week calls.

**Both views agree on Week 4 = 5 calls/week.** That's the target. Lock it.

## What Happens at Week 8

Two paths from the July 5 review:

**Path A — Signed**: $25K in. Reference rights secured. Mobilize for delivery (decide what gives — what's deprioritized to service the design partner). Decide whether to pursue vertical #2 in parallel or finish vertical #1 first. New KPI = expansion + delivery quality.

**Path B — Not signed**: Hard reset conversation with Yoda. What's the most honest read on why? Wrong vertical? Wrong offer? Wrong motion? Wrong product? Pick one diagnosis (don't try to fix five things) and reset the next 4-week test.

**The actually-bad outcome**: 8 weeks pass and we don't *know* which path we're on because data wasn't tracked or was tracked dishonestly. This doc's job is to prevent that.

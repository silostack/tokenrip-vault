### What "Discovery" Means

A discovery call is NOT a demo. It's a 20-30 minute conversation where we:
1. Ask what their current workflow looks like (specific, not general)
2. Ask what breaks, what takes the most time, what they hate
3. Ask what they've tried to fix it
4. Ask what it costs them (time, money, missed revenue, staff turnover)
5. Listen for judgment-heavy flows — the stuff that can't be automated with a Zap

**Output of a discovery call**: A written pain signal with specifics. *"Agency principal at [Firm] (22 employees) spends 30+ CSR hours/week on COI processing because there's no tool that automates the review step."* That's a pain signal. *"They think AI could help with efficiency"* is NOT a pain signal.

## Decision Points

These are the moments where we stop and recalibrate. Pre-commit to them now so we don't drift.

### End of Week 2 of outreach (Sun May 24)
**Question**: Is outbound math working?
- ✅ At least 3 calls booked from week 2 outreach → continue
- ⚠️ 0-2 calls booked → diagnose: ICP wrong, message wrong, or volume too low
- 🛑 0 connection acceptances → ICP or profile fundamentally wrong; pause and rethink

### End of Week 4 of outreach (Sun June 7)
**Question**: Is the motion producing real conversations?
- ✅ 5+ calls/week sustained, at least 1 demo conversation per week → continue
- ⚠️ Calls happening but not converting to demos → discovery script weak; Simon helps refine
- 🛑 No movement past first call → product/positioning problem; pause to rethink

### End of Week 6 of outreach (Sun June 21)
**Question**: Is anyone close to signing?
- ✅ 1+ prospect in active term sheet conversation → push to close
- ⚠️ Prospects say "interesting but not now" → diagnose timing, urgency, or fit issue
- 🛑 No prospect has asked for terms → offer or product fundamentally not landing

### End of Week 8 of outreach (Sun July 5)
**Question**: Did we sign one?
- ✅ Yes → mobilize for delivery; reflect on what worked; plan vertical #2 timing
- 🛑 No → hard reset. What's the lesson? Different vertical? Different motion? Different offer? Or different product?

## Hard Rules

These are commitments to avoid known traps.

1. **No demo before discovery.** Don't build a demo until discovery calls validate the pain. Outreach starts without a demo — curiosity-driven messaging only. Demo gets scoped to what you heard, not what you assumed.
2. **Pain is the metric.** Every weekly review asks: "What pain did we hear this week?" If the answer is "none," something is wrong — messaging, targeting, or vertical.
3. **No custom features for prospects pre-signature.** "We could build that for you" is sales magic in conversation. It also creates expectations you'll regret. Hold the line: in-scope is in-scope, out-of-scope is paid.
4. **No pilot under $15K.** Free pilots produce no urgency, no learning. Even if we discount heavily, charge enough that they show up.
5. **No vertical change for 2 weeks.** Verticals locked until May 23. After that, data-driven adjustment only.
6. **No "we'll figure it out later" on IP and reference rights.** These are non-negotiable in every conversation, every contract.

## What Success Looks Like at Week 8

**Best case**: One signed design partner. Cash in. Reference rights secured. Building the vertical stack for them. Vertical #2 selected and prep started.

**Realistic case**: One serious term-sheet conversation in flight. 2-3 prospects in late-stage pipeline. Clear evidence the motion is working. Probably 2-4 weeks from first close.

**Worst case**: No close, no late-stage conversation. But: clear data on what's failing (ICP / message / vertical / offer / product). The cost of testing wrong was 8 weeks of focused work — that's a fast learn cycle.

The actually-bad case is **scattered effort, no signal, no conviction at week 8.** That's what these docs and rules are designed to prevent.

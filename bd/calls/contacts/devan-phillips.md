## Running Intelligence

**Firm mechanics (per Devan, broker framing):**
- **"A larger broker"** — finds the loan, brokers it, takes a cut from the lender. (⚠️ contradicts the prep's "balance-sheet direct lender" — reconcile.)
- **Comp/volume:** measured by gross margin to the company; ~**$120K/agent/yr** gross-margin target. **7 direct agents** + "quite a few" outside agents bringing deals; Devan is the one who manages the outside agents.
- **Deal size:** focus minimum **$20K** (will do $10K one-offs to land a vendor); average ~**$100K**; caps ~**$2M** (options higher "including through you guys" — i.e., places larger deals out).
- **Speed:** 650+ FICO / 3+ yrs in business → **approval in 1-2 business days**; subpar (~600 FICO or ~1 yr in business) → ~a week. Funding ~another week after terms agreed. **Max ~2 weeks.**
- **Internal bottleneck:** the **credit team** does initial underwriting and "fine-tooth-combs the banks" ("we just need three months of bank statements"). Gets bogged down over holiday weekends.
- **Lender-side drag:** lenders slow-walk **lower-credit** files (back-burner behind A-credit).

**Devan's origination channels:** LinkedIn + Facebook marketing; local **BNI** networking group; **vendor relationships** (e.g., an **agriculture-drone dealer** = ~5 deals). End users + vendors + brokering to lenders.

**The pain Devan actually named (vs. the pain Stauss built on top of it):**
- *Devan's words:* (1) **filtering unqualified applicants** — active bankruptcies/judgments/collections — "a couple times a week," slows the process; (2) **bank-statement analysis** — auto-compute average monthly deposits over 3-6 months and gate ("$85K → good; $10K → no"); (3) wants to **move faster** (already fast; aspirational).
- *Stauss amplified these into:* a **1-10 deal-scoring/qualifier agent**, a **"100% clean file" → lender** to compress approval/funding to 24-72 hrs, and lender-ranking/prospecting. **Watch: most of the vision is Stauss's framing, not Devan's articulated pain** (recurrence of "insider authors the wedge," insights 2026-05-29).

## Running Intelligence

**VFI mechanics (insider):**
- Direct lessor with **warehouse-facility capital limits** and **per-industry exposure buckets** (e.g. filled the trucking bucket after ~$50M in trucking in a down year). Frees capital via **securitization (ABS)** — "move money into the buckets" — which gates how much new business they can fund.
- **Funding-speed cost:** yield accrues from first wire. A $5M deal can't fund until 2–3 days out (submission-window-gated); missing the window adds another day. Aggregate per-diem drag across the book = "tens to hundreds of thousands… millions" in foregone yield. (This is the crypto-overflow pain — funding/treasury, not cleanly AI-addressable without crypto.)
- **Differentiator Stauss claims:** VFI can include soft/intangible assets (e.g. GPU components banks treat as soft cost) in an equipment facility — "I've never met anyone like VFI who can do the full package." Targets CFOs with blanket approvals ("$20M this year, use it on whatever equipment + soft costs your bank won't finance").

**Concrete VFI ops pain (real, painkiller-grade):**
- **Redline / documentation cycle:** agreements transferred back and forth, notations made manually. With vacations / people out, a redline takes "two weeks to four months"; deals go stale 2–5 weeks. ← strongest, most quantified pain in the call.
- **Financial due-diligence prep:** ~3 hours to assemble a DD package per deal.
- **Credit memo / IC summary prep:** Alek's pre-call read = "the big one, good starting point." Analyst grunt-work.

**Growth ideas Stauss is hottest on (vitamin/greenfield, where his enthusiasm lives):**
- **Targeted origination signal-finder:** surface USA cos $50M+ rev with $5M+ annual capex *and the timing of the need* — connect to the CFO when the deal is live. Tier-2/tier-3 AI-data-center supply chain (racking, liquid cooling, GPUs) as the hot vertical.
- **Off-lease equipment IQ tracker** (his personal side project): upload warranty/insurance docs, track usage/optimal utilization, recommend upgrade/repair/replace, then layer financing. He'd brand this as an independent "all-things-equipment advisor" business.

**Market intel:**
- ~several hundred mid-tier direct lenders / ABL firms (the "VFIs, SLR Capitals") move tens-to-hundreds of millions weekly — an addressable cohort below the publicly-traded private funds (Blue Owl, Ares).
- Trucking sector took an 18-month hit; bankruptcies, weak interim financials even on good profiles.
- VFI culture: high autonomy, no rep monitoring, no standardized process ("you have it or you don't"). One sales manager is a heavy AI user. **Implication:** no top-down process to automate; adoption would be bottom-up, champion-led — which cuts against an enterprise platform sale and toward per-seat / per-rep tooling.

## Running Intelligence

- **Offshore labor arbitrage undercuts AI cost savings pitch.** 5 employees at $1K/month total. The "reduce headcount" value prop is dead on arrival. Luai explicitly: "Five of them cost me a thousand dollars, which is nothing. I pay $5,000 rent."
- **IRCC portal requires human submission.** Cannot automate government portal applications. PDF forms can be automated; portal entries cannot. This is a hard constraint that limits AI scope to pre-submission work.
- **AI credibility gap with Middle Eastern/Indian clients.** Luai's clients are disproportionately from regions where AI trust is low. If an AI asks a question and then a human asks a different question, it "decreases credibility." His word: "until today they don't understand the AI concept."
- **"Zero mistakes" bar for case work.** Luai's standard is no mistakes on completeness checks — analogous to IRCC's own standard of returning applications for missing signatures, unstamped documents, etc. He wants all issues surfaced "in one shot, not back and forth."
- **Document quality is a real blocker.** Clients submit phone photos as PDFs instead of scans. OCR/AI errors on poor-quality documents create downstream mistakes.
- **Phone > email for immigration sales.** Fraud prevalence in immigration services means clients won't pay based on email alone. Human voice is trust signal. "Nobody will pay money just for receiving an email."
- **B2B back-office model economics**: Per-case pricing ($200-$300 on a $1K case, $3K-$4K on a $7K-$8K case) or dedicated case manager ($1.5K-$2K/month, costs him $200-$300). Targeting other immigration consultants. This is a scale play built on his trained offshore team.

## Alek's Performance

### Coaching Priorities

1. **VFI name-drop — competitor hygiene violation.** Alek said "we started co-building with VFI, which is another direct lender — they're more high ticket, over in Utah." Said it twice (once in the origin story too). The demo prep explicitly warned: Empire's CM desk trades with peer institutions, making Empire effectively a VFI competitor. Naming VFI tells Katharine that her competitor's data/criteria have been inside the system. **Better language:** *"We started by co-building with a direct lender — they had the same sourcing pain you're describing."* Why it matters: if Mike or Rick Google VFI and realize it's a competitor, trust erodes. Fix this before the team call.

2. **"Just a mock" undermined the underwriting demo.** Alek said "this is just a mock" when showing the underwrite. This frames the product as vaporware. Katharine's response was polite but she immediately said "the underwrite part, I don't know how interesting it is" — the mock framing gave her permission to dismiss it. **Better language:** *"This is a working build — same engine, we'd configure it to your criteria. What you'd get back would look like this but with your deal, your box."* Why it matters: the underwriting is the most-built part of Quintel and it was presented as the least credible.

3. **Vague on sources when she asked directly.** Katharine asked "what kinds of sources do you use?" — the single most important technical question. Alek said "filings, UCC, LinkedIn posts." The prep had: SEC filings/EDGAR, federal contract awards/USAspending, UCC liens, regulatory forcing functions, sponsor activity, grid/utility interconnection. A crisp list signals depth; a vague one signals early-stage guessing. **Better language:** *"SEC filings — we read the 8-Ks that name private counterparties inside public disclosures. Federal contract awards from USAspending. UCC lien maturities for refi timing. Regulatory triggers like EPA 2027. And sponsor activity — portfolio acquisitions and expansions."* Why it matters: she was leaning in and ready to be impressed; a specific answer would have locked the "this is real" impression.

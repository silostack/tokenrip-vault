## Alek's Performance

### Coaching Priorities

1. **Kept pitching operational efficiency after the prospect said operations are covered.** Gautham said "not really much in our space" re: AI needs, disclosed his own CRM, and mentioned Keepik — three separate signals that operational tooling is not the opening. Alek's response: "So the biggest bottleneck is essentially the leads. And then if you're able to solve that, you can take on way more clients." That's a correct diagnosis of the pain but then Alek offered to "build a solution for that" — implying operational AI when the pain is marketing. **Better language:** After hearing "marketing is the biggest challenge," the honest response is: *"Marketing and lead conversion is a different beast from what we typically build. Our sweet spot is the behind-the-scenes work — intake, document prep, application review. If your ops are handled, it sounds like you're ahead of most firms we've talked to. Is there anywhere in the conversion funnel where the bottleneck is information processing rather than persuasion?"* This either finds a genuine opening or ends the call honestly. **Why it matters:** Offering to build a solution for a pain you can't actually address wastes the prospect's time and erodes credibility for future interactions.

2. **Math was wrong on revenue loss quantification.** Alek said "you're losing $20,000 a case" — but Gautham just said fees are $2-3K per case. The math should have been: 10-25% of ~40-50 leads/month at $2-3K = $8K-$37.5K/month. Instead Alek anchored on a $20K number that came from nowhere and was 10x the stated case value. Gautham didn't correct it, which means either he was disengaged or too polite to push back. **Better practice:** Repeat back the prospect's own numbers: *"So at $2-3K per case, and 10-25% lost — that's $8K-$35K per month walking away. Over a year that's six figures."* Using their numbers shows you listened. Making up numbers shows you didn't. **Why it matters:** Wrong math in a sales call is a trust-killer. Even if the prospect doesn't flag it, they notice.

3. **Didn't qualify early enough — should have asked about existing tech stack in the first 3 minutes.** The in-house CRM, the Keepik free tool, and the Hyderabad IT team all came out organically mid-call. If Alek had asked "What tools are you currently using for your workflow?" at the start, the call would have been 10 minutes instead of 25, and Alek could have either found a real gap or moved on. **Better language:** After the intro pitch, *"Before I go deeper — what are you using now for CRM, compliance, and document prep? I don't want to pitch you something you've already built."* **Why it matters:** Early qualification saves both parties time and builds credibility (it shows you're looking for fit, not just pitching).

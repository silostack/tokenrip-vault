## Call Summary

Alek ran a firm-direct discovery call with Luai Walid El Haj, founder of Get In Canada (Montreal immigration consultancy). Luai revealed he already has extensive automation (AI-powered assessment/eligibility system, twice-daily IRCC policy scraping) and 5 offshore employees at $1K/month total. He is not in acute pain — he's comparison shopping. He's skeptical of AI for client-facing case work due to credibility risks with Middle Eastern/Indian clients and poor document quality. He requested a detailed email with services and pricing before agreeing to a second meeting. Late in the call, he disclosed a B2B back-office venture where he'd prep files for other immigration consultants — potentially a more interesting angle for Tokenrip.

## Momentum

→ **Flat.** Luai has a working system and cheap labor. He's willing to look at alternatives but isn't compelled. The email deliverable is a test: if it's vague or generic, the relationship dies. If it demonstrates specific immigration workflow knowledge and clear pricing, the door stays open.

## Key Intelligence / What Changed

1. **Offshore labor arbitrage kills the cost-savings pitch for small immigration firms.** $1K/month for 5 trained employees means AI needs to deliver capability, not savings. This likely applies across the immigration vertical — many firms outsource to low-cost countries.

2. **The questionnaire → form → verification workflow is the clearest AI insertion point.** Luai described a concrete, painful workflow: receive client questionnaire → check for completeness → cross-reference against documents (passport spelling, dates) → follow up on missing info → transfer to official forms/portal. This is document intelligence + completeness checking, not portal automation.

3. **B2B immigration back-office is a real market.** Luai is building a human-powered version: prep files for consultants at $200-$4K per case or $1.5K-$2K/month for a dedicated case manager. His cost basis is $200-$300/month per person. If 20 consultants sign up, that's $30K+ MRR. Tokenrip could be the AI layer that makes each case manager 3-5x more productive, enabling him to scale without linearly adding headcount.

## Alek's Performance

### Coaching Priorities

1. **Led with "reduce headcount" to someone who morally objects to firing employees.** Alek said: "potentially to help you replace that and reduce... potentially even reduce your headcount." Luai immediately pushed back with his ethical stance on keeping employees. **Better language:** "Your team is clearly efficient. The question is whether each person could handle more volume with better tooling — so you grow revenue without growing cost." **Why it matters:** Headcount reduction is a toxic frame for firms that view employees as moral obligations. Read the room on this — especially with immigrant-background founders who often have strong communal values.

2. **No pricing or specifics ready when asked.** Luai asked directly: "What's the scope of your services and how much does it cost?" Alek's answer was vague ("our scope's super wide"). When Luai asked again at the end, Alek still couldn't deliver. **Better language:** Have 2-3 concrete packages with price ranges ready for every discovery call. Even ballpark numbers ("our typical immigration firm engagement starts at $X/month") demonstrate seriousness. **Why it matters:** Luai explicitly said "just mentioned we do this and that, not automation" — he wants specifics. Vagueness reads as early-stage or unserious.

3. **Missed the B2B back-office opportunity entirely.** Luai described a concrete business he's building — a back-office service for other immigration consultants. Alek's response: "off the top of my head, AI allows you to increase the outputs of all your employees." This was the most interesting angle of the entire call and it got a throwaway line. **Better language:** "Wait — you're building a back-office service for other consultants? That's exactly the kind of business our infrastructure is designed to power. How many consultants are you targeting? What if each of your case managers could handle 3x the files with AI assistance — that changes your unit economics completely." **Why it matters:** Luai's direct pain is mild. But his B2B venture has real scale potential, and AI-augmented case managers is a fundamentally different (and stronger) value prop than "replace your $200/month employees."

### What Worked

1. **Good discovery question on follow-up sequences** (09:11). Probed the dead-lead re-engagement process, which surfaced real workflow detail about their sales cadence and promotion automation.

2. **"Human in the loop" framing was the right instinct** (18:14-18:51). When Luai pushed back on AI doing client-facing work, Alek correctly pivoted to "AI prepares, human reviews" and gave a concrete time comparison (30 minutes vs. 5-10 hours). This was the strongest moment of the call.

3. **Phased rollout suggestion** (20:28). The "draft first, then auto-send once you're confident" framing directly addressed Luai's credibility concern. This is the right go-to-market for skeptical buyers.

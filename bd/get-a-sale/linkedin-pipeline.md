# LinkedIn Warm Pipeline — Playbook

*Active as of 2026-05-23. Simon-owned. Goal: manufacture a Simon-owned most-progressable deal so "0 → 1" doesn't land in Alek's column only (Luai).*

## The Motion

Convert accepted connections into real conversations. Not cold outreach — the cold leg already happened in the discovery sprint (LinkedIn-insurance, killed 2026-05-18). This is the *warm follow-up* on connections that actually accepted.

## What "real conversation" means

A real conversation:
- Surfaces a stated problem the person owns
- Is specific enough to evaluate against the substrate-yield filter (forward-deployed engineer — would solving this build reusable substrate?)
- Has a path to a next step the person agrees to (call, problem write-up, intro)

A "nice-to-meet-you" exchange is not a conversation. A back-and-forth without a path forward is not a conversation.

## Follow-up sequence

1. **First touch (post-acceptance)**: Short, specific, no pitch. Reference something concrete from their profile or recent activity. Open with curiosity about a problem area.
2. **Discovery hook**: If they engage, ask one specific question about a workflow problem. (We're not selling architecture — we're listening for a problem that solving builds substrate.)
3. **Call ask**: 20-min discovery call. Frame as "I'm researching how teams in [their area] handle [problem]" — not "I'd like to demo our product."

## Filters

At zero revenue:
- Almost nothing is filtered out. Custom builds produce substrate yield. Take the call.
- Decline only on obvious time-sinks: tire-kickers, gatekeeper-only contacts, fundraising solicitations, agency-style pitches *to* us.

Filters tighten with revenue. Hard-filter at ~$1M ARR. Until then, the bar is "is there a problem worth solving and someone willing to pay for it?"

## Watch

- **Quality over count.** "X connections messaged" is a vanity number. The metric is conversations had + deals materially advanced.
- **Architecture-talk drift.** If a call starts to be about architecture, it's because the person is unusual (Vijay-pattern). That's fine — but don't lead there. Lead with their problem.
- **Vitamin signals.** "Interesting, send me more" without a next step is a vitamin signal. Note it; don't chase.

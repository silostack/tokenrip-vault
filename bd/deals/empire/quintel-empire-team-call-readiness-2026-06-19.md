# Quintel — Empire Team Call · Close-Readiness Plan

> **What this is.** Katharine (Empire Asset Finance) committed to circulating materials and setting up a broader team call with Rick (CEO), Mike (COO/CCO), and Aaron (VP Orig.). This doc defines what it takes for Simon + Alek to be **close-ready** when that call happens — the outcome to aim for, who's in the room, what to demo, pricing, objections to pre-load, roles, and the checklist. Deal context (customer, stack, stage, commitments) lives in the companion scope doc and the contact file; this doc does not repeat it.

---

## 1. The target outcome

The team call is a **multi-buyer call**, not a repeat of Katharine's. **She is already sold.** The call exists to convert the people she cannot close on her own — **Rick (ROI / vendor-risk) and Mike (credit accuracy / data security).**

**Close-ready = walking out with a verbal yes + a design-partner term sheet to sign** — not "great, we'll discuss internally." Every item below ladders to that single outcome. If a piece of prep doesn't move the room toward a signature that day, it's polish, not prep.

---

## 2. The room — map each seat to what it needs

| Who | Lens | What they need to hear / see | Owns it |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Katharine** | Champion / sourcing | Nothing new to sell — *arm* her, don't re-pitch her. Don't bore the champion. | — |
| **Rick (CEO)** | ROI · why now · vendor risk | Cost, return, and why a 9-month-old lender bets on a young vendor. Use her own "be early, not year-seven scrambling" frame. | Alek |
| **Mike (COO/CCO, JD — the F2 user)** | Credit accuracy · does it actually underwrite · where does our data live | The redacted deal run — *his* deal, read correctly, with provenance. Whether this rides alongside or displaces F2. Security/PII answer. | **Simon** |
| **Aaron (VP Orig.)** | Sourcing (same as Katharine) | The sourcing story; a second user vote | Alek |

**The hardest seat is Mike, and it's Simon's.** A credit officer with a JD whose tool (F2) is degrading will ask "is the underwrite *right*?" and "where does our data live?" Simon must be in the room and field those cold. Alek discovers and frames; Simon carries technical-buyer credibility. **Do not run this call Alek-solo.**

---

## 3. The demo — one coherent surface, their deal as the centerpiece

- **Centerpiece — their own redacted deal, run live through the real engine.** Extraction with provenance (every number traced to its source doc) is the beat that beats Mike's skepticism and Katharine's prospecting-GPT. Running *their* closed deal back at them — "here's what our engine produced from the file you already underwrote" — is the most persuasive move available, and it's the packet already in flight (see scope doc).
- **Second beat — a synthetic Empire-box sourcing artifact.** Katharine's hot button is sourcing; the redacted run doesn't show it. Have a signal feed scoped to her buy box ready as the "and it also does this" moment, so the room sees both halves of the product.
- **Keep the underwriting judgment moldable, not polished.** A too-perfect score triggers Mike's "that's not how I'd price it." A rough, co-creatable read triggers "I'd weight X differently" — which *is* the close starting.

---

## 4. Pricing — have a real number, and do not give it away

Katharine asked pricing unprompted (setup fee + monthly, 2–4 users). Empire is a **$100M-backed institutional buyer with real budget** — not an Upwork pilot. **The trap is "design partner = free." Wrong instinct here.**

Design-partner *concessions* should be **reference rights + roadmap input + direct build access — not a discount to zero.**

**Posture to lock before the call:**
- **Setup fee** covering ingestion + config + box authoring (the real work) — a meaningful institutional number, not $5K.
- **Monthly** per the usage/seat model she already expects.
- **Design-partner trade:** they get priority build + co-creation; we get a logo, a reference, and a **signed reference-rights letter at kickoff.**

Lock exact figures against the gameplan's pricing rules before the call. **The point: walk in with a number you'll say out loud.** Hesitating on price to an institutional buyer reads as "we've never sold this" — which feeds the vendor-risk objection.

> **Decision to settle first (gates the term sheet + demo emphasis):** paid design partner vs. free pilot. Recommendation: **paid design partner.** ONE thing is *get a sale*; a $100M-fund buyer who asked pricing unprompted should pay.

---

## 5. Objections to pre-load (predictable, all answerable)

1. **"Do we keep F2?"** — she raised it twice. Ride *alongside* first, displace as trust builds. Don't force a rip-out; let the clean-queue effect make the case.
2. **Security / data residency** — institutional lender, Arena parent. Mike *will* ask where data lives, who sees it, PII handling. Have a one-paragraph answer ready. **Simon's.**
3. **Vendor risk** — "you're a small startup; we'd bet our origination function on you." Counter with the design-partner frame (we build *for* them, they shape it) + Katharine's own "be early" logic.
4. **Custom-vs-product** — Katharine's language leans "a tool we scale with"; the first call pitched done-for-you. Rick will want to know if he's buying a product or hiring a consultant. **FDE answer: a product they adopt, that we deploy for them.** Land that framing consistently — Alek and Simon must describe it the same way in the room.
5. **HubSpot integration** — she called a plugin a hard requirement (two-way: scrub tier-1s, add net-new). Have a credible integration story even if it's roadmap, not built. (Scope-doc Commitment #5.)

---

## 6. CoI hygiene — non-negotiable

Empire's capital-markets desk trades with peers → **Empire is effectively a VFI competitor.** Alek name-dropped VFI twice on the first call. Everything in the room and in the packet must be **synthetic or Empire's own** — zero VFI box, criteria, partners, or war stories. **Brief Alek on VFI silence before the call.**

---

## 7. Roles

- **Alek leads and closes** — framing, ROI to Rick, sourcing to Katharine/Aaron, the ask.
- **Simon carries Mike** — credit-accuracy credibility, security/data residency, the live demo on the real surface.
- Both describe the product the *same way* (product they adopt + we deploy — §5.4).

---

## 8. "Maximally ready" — the close-readiness checklist

By the time the call happens, in a perfect world:

- [ ] **Their redacted deal runs live through the real Quintel desk** (not a mock, not a different URL) — provenance extraction working
- [ ] **Synthetic Empire-box sourcing artifact** ready as the second beat
- [ ] **One coherent product surface** decided — the same thing they'll buy
- [ ] **Pricing locked** — setup + monthly numbers you'll say out loud
- [ ] **Design-partner term sheet drafted** and ready to send/sign that day (+ reference-rights letter)
- [ ] **The explicit ask scripted** — what "yes" looks like at the end of the call
- [ ] **Objection answers ready** — F2, security, vendor risk, custom-vs-product, HubSpot
- [ ] **Roles set** — Alek leads + closes, Simon carries Mike (credit + security)
- [ ] **Alek briefed on VFI silence**

**The two long poles that gate everything and aren't started:**
1. **The live demo on the real surface** — depends on the desk-surface build state (the §6 unknown in the scope doc).
2. **The term sheet + pricing** — depends on settling the paid-vs-free posture (§4).

Everything else is hours of work once those two are decided.

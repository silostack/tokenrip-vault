## Slide 3 — The substrate

**Purpose**: Name what Tokenrip is (stands alone), how it spreads, and how it makes money.

**Headline**: Tokenrip is the GitHub for operational agent work.

- **What it is:** versioning, review, provenance, permissions, outcome capture — live across REST, CLI, MCP.
- **The mapping is structural:** artifacts→commits · forking→branches · review→PRs · **lineage→blame.**
- **How it spreads:** we deploy into a vertical and ship the acting agent; the substrate forms underneath. (Palantir built Foundry this way — not cold sales.)

*Dashboard free, acting agent paid — and we never pay for inference, so margins expand with scale.*

> Emphasize "lineage→blame" — it sets up the moat. The italic line is the monetization + economics beat in one.

## Slide 4 — Proof

**Purpose**: The generalization claim, proven by two maximally different verticals. The only present-tense evidence — it carries the most weight.

**Headline**: If it works for a lender and a hospital, it works anywhere.

- **Equipment finance (Quintel)** — live dashboard, built with a 20-yr industry partner. A deal touches 5+ parties; its history scatters across 3 silos. *The broker never learns what funded.*
- **Healthcare credentialing (AICAP)** — paid discovery done, MVP next. A case ricochets between provider, verification, and committee. *The trail lives in email, not a ledger.*
- **Same primitives, zero rebuild:** extraction, ingestion, rubric-as-config scoring, operator review, provenance logging.

> Status, plainly (callout, not hero): *deployed dashboard + paid discovery + booked pipeline; the acting-agent + why-loop ships next — by config, not rebuild.* The two italic punch-lines are a deliberate parallel ("never learns what funded" / "lives in email") — keep them rhyming. Honesty: EF dashboard is live; credentialing is in build — the headline claims *generalization*, not that both are deployed.

## Slide 5 — The moat

**Purpose**: The reframe. The moat isn't the dashboard or the outcome — it's the recorded *reasoning.* Load-bearing slide.

**Headline**: We're building a data moat out of *why* — the reasoning behind every decision.

- **The why-graph:** every deal and case records what changed and *why* — git-blame for operational work. Proprietary; can't be reconstructed from outside.
- **It compounds:** the reasoning feeds back into scoring — the product gets smarter with use and harder to leave.
- **The network:** multi-party work, one agent identity across verticals — depth per vertical, network across all.

**Because the *why* is the same primitive everywhere, operational work converges on one substrate — the way every codebase converged on git.**

> Three points, then the bold convergence line. **For the conversation, not the slide:** the cross-party network is architecture + early evidence today, not demonstrated at scale — don't claim mature network usage.

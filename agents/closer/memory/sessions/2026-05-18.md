## Decisions & Assessments

- **No org-structure label.** Don't call it an agency or a startup. Write in first person ("I"), let a shipped product (rebelfi.io) carry credibility. That combination is the real "middle ground" — personal-voice warmth + proof of delivery — and needs zero positioning words.
- **No case-study discount in cold proposals.** Offering a case-study discount signals hunger and shifts the proposal to our agenda, not the buyer's outcome (§2). It's a closing lever for the call, never a cold-proposal line.
- **No free bespoke POC.** Same hunger tell. A meaningful crypto fintech POC needs the buyer's context anyway. The no-commitment demo already exists: tokenrip.com/agents (live, viewable with zero contact). The tailored walkthrough belongs on the call, after the buyer has shown up. Paid 1-2 day discovery (§5) is the right de-risk for $5K+ deals — hold for the call.
- **CTA: question primary, call secondary.** A call is higher-friction than a one-line question. Lead with an easy scoping question; offer the call as the "if it's easier" alternative. Loses nobody.

## Codified — Opening Posture Rubric (playbook §10)

Simon flagged that the "the hard part of your build is X" opener felt off and asked to codify why. Diagnosis: it **asserted authority over the client's own problem** — bidder-as-teacher, client-as-student, an inverted status relationship. Even a correct insight lands as a lecture when delivered that way.

The fix (Simon's rewrite): swap to **checked understanding** — "If I'm reading this right, you're looking for X." Proves comprehension, keeps the client as the authority, invites correction, doubles as a soft CTA. Three postures, only the third works: flat restate (template), authoritative assertion (presumptuous), checked understanding (correct). Sequence rule: humble comprehension first, expertise second — the same insight reads as a lecture in sentence one and as competence in sentence three.

Codified as playbook §10. §2, §7, §8, and the AIDA skeleton updated to reference it; §7 rewritten from "text message, 3-6 sentences" to "cover letter, composed professional register."

## Patterns Observed

- **Hunger tells cluster.** Case-study discounts and free POCs both come from the same impulse — "give them a reason to pick me." In a cold proposal both backfire: they signal low value of our time and shift focus off the buyer's outcome. The fix is always the same: the live link is the no-risk proof; bespoke/discounted offers wait for the call. Added to patterns.md.
- **Proof framing is buyer-specific.** The same credential (RebelFi) gets reframed per buyer: crypto detail forward for a crypto buyer, stripped to generic "fintech" for a non-crypto buyer where the jargon is noise or risk. Added to patterns.md.

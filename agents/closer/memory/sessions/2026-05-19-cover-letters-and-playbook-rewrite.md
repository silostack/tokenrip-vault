## The big shift: playbook rewrite

Simon rejected the AIDA scaffolding and the "checked-understanding" opening outright.
His words: leading with "you're looking for X" is ceremony and a bad start;
"you asked for..." is parroting. He asked for a first-principles rethink.

**Changes made to `upwork-proposal-playbook.md` (full rewrite):**
- **AIDA dropped entirely.** Old §8 (AIDA structure) and the AIDA skeleton are gone.
- **"Checked-understanding" open retired.** The "if I'm reading this right..." hedge
  is dead — it reads as ceremony and, on clear jobs, as unsure of the obvious.
- **New §2** — "What Makes a Great Cover Letter": seven first-principles principles
  (no ceremony, specificity over narration, show don't claim, one clickable proof,
  have a spine, short plain voice, leading close).
- **New §3** — "Open on Substance, No Ceremony": open with who you are (one concrete
  relevant sentence) OR a real point of view that reframes the job. Never narrate
  the post back, never "you asked for."
- Sections renumbered for clean logical order (§4 proof, §5 impl, §6 discovery,
  §7 close, §8 length/structure, §9 humanize). §9 stays humanize (command ref intact).
- `patterns.md` cross-references updated (§5→§6, §6→§7, §10→§3) and the retired
  checked-understanding language fixed.

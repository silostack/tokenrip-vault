# Substrate GTM: Wrappers, Funnels, and Deposited Brains

**Status**: developing
**Created**: 2026-06-11
**Last touched**: 2026-06-11

## Thesis

Infrastructure can't be sold; it has to be wrapped — nobody consumer-adopted Postgres. Tokenrip's GTM decomposes into one repeated sentence with different buyers: *somebody wraps the substrate in a nameable product, and a workspace gets deposited that compounds.* Agents are the funnel, the brain is the product, autonomy and multiplayer are the paywalls. Four wrappers: consumer agent catalog, FDE leave-behind, Cowork companion, agency channel.

## Evolution

- **2026-06-11**: Emerged from Simon's GTM question (is there a grassroots/consumer motion? is there a "walk-forward" B2B motion where Tokenrip plays a bigger role than the current walk-backward FDE pattern?). ## Key Challenges Surfaced

- **LinkedIn automation is a ToS gray zone with spam-adjacent brand smell** — the first wedge sets the brand. Resolution: human-in-the-loop version (agent researches/drafts/queues; human sends); sellable autonomy becomes overnight prep, not auto-send. *Resolved in design, untested in market.*
- **One video ≠ a motion** — the consumer play is a cadence business, a standing marketing muscle distinct from delivery work. The artifact-shipping format is the best version of that cost (content compounds into catalog). *Open — honest cost, named.*
- **GPT-store corpse-smell** — a packaged agent is only a product if it has real state/tools/schedule. Catalog discipline: small and deep (5 compounding agents) over wide and shallow (50 wrappers). *Open as discipline.*
- **Leave-behind blocker check** — is anything actually preventing current engagements from delivering visibly on-substrate, or has it just not been framed that way? *Open — possibly free.*

## Open Questions

- Agency channel: what's the minimum platform surface an agency needs (tenanting, white-label, client visibility) before this is sellable?
- Cowork companion pricing: per-workspace flat vs. per-seat?

## Non-Obvious Connections

- **Outreach passes the requires-the-architecture test** — an outreach agent without persistent state is broken on day two (re-prospects, forgets what converted). A custom GPT structurally can't do the job. Demand pre-exists (Expandi/Dripify/HeyReach spend) — category upgrade, not category education.
- **The autonomy paywall operationalizes "autonomy, not construction, is the leverage unlock" (2026-06-04)** — people pay at exactly the moment the agent stops being a chore and runs overnight. Free to drive, pay for autopilot.
- **The catalog is the hero/lure synthesis (2026-04-27) industrialized** — each lure (packaged agent riding pre-existing demand) feeds the hero (the accumulating workspace brain).
- **The moa skill + bootloader pattern are the packaging infrastructure already built** — the build video is a moa session on camera.
- **Leave-behind = "the brain as deliverable"** from [[semantic-workspaces-multiplayer]] — the deliverable is the lock-in; year-2 platform renewal is the revenue shape (Palantir: FDE in, Foundry stays).
- **Agency channel = selling the dogfood** — the extraction engine, deal screens, and leave-behind pattern being built for Tokenrip's own FDE work *are* the playbook an agency would buy. Make.com's partner-led growth is the precedent (ties to the P1 Make.com playbook analysis).
- **"Bring your spreadsheet" onboarding** — anchor to existing artifacts (2026-05-10 insight): importing the user's manual-outreach data is both onboarding and lock-in seed.

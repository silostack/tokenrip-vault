# Workplace Bottom-Up Agent Adoption

**Status**: developing
**Created**: 2026-05-14
**Last touched**: 2026-05-14

## Thesis

The investable angle for Tokenrip — particularly for the a16z accelerator pitch — is to swap the protagonist from "creator-with-audience" to "non-technical employee automating themselves with AI." This is the bottom-up SaaS pattern (Slack/Notion/Figma/GitHub) happening right now in AI agents. The mounted-agent architecture is unchanged; what changes is the story.

The protagonist is Glo: a non-developer arts-degree worker automating her job with ChatGPT walking her through it, working in VS Code terminal, boss-supported, explicitly refusing to use OpenClaw at work ("gonna give me a virus"), without Enterprise Anthropic. She's patient zero of a new species — the knowledge worker whose primary mode of work is "tell AI what I do and have it automate me." Every one of her automations dies on her laptop. There is no path from individual workflow to team capability.

The three-step ladder:
1. **Glo** (free, individual wedge) — mounts agents in her own ChatGPT/VS Code/Claude. Workflows persist.
2. **AI Agent Manager** (first paying buyer) — coordinates the 3-8 Glos on her team. Buys the team plan to share, version, and govern the workflow library.
3. **Company Tenant** (enterprise plan) — runs the MCP layer on their own infrastructure. Connects internal data sources. Becomes the substrate for company-wide automation.

Each step is load-bearing on mounted-agent architecture (portability, persistence, observability, BYO model, memory). Each step is precedented by Slack-pattern bottom-up SaaS adoption a16z already believes in.

## Why This Beats the Creator Pitch for a16z

- **Protagonist is concrete** (Glo) rather than abstract (mid-tier creator).
- **Motion is precedented** (bottom-up workplace SaaS) rather than novel (audience-led deploys).
- **"Why now" has a non-circular answer** — top-down enterprise AI is being sold; bottom-up worker AI is happening; the gap has no home.
- **Dissolves the creator-economy framing trap** — creators become supply, not protagonist.
- **VC pattern-match is favorable** — Slack/Notion/Figma/GitHub all started exactly here.
- **Requires only one novel belief** (bottom-up agent adoption is happening), not two (mounted agents + audience-led).

## What Doesn't Change

- The architecture (mounted-agent imprint/memory/harness separation)
- BYO model economics
- Tokenrip product surface
- The five-layer roadmap
- Series 3 content thesis (becomes the supply-side education layer)

## What Does Change

- Glo is the slide-2 protagonist, not the creator with audience.
- Creator motion becomes the supply layer (where workflow content comes from).
- GTM emphasizes workplace discovery (Upwork gigs, Glo-class talk-tracks) over creator outreach.
- Enterprise tenant becomes the long-term moat slide, not a parallel motion.
- AI Agent Manager becomes the named buyer persona at the team-paid tier.

## Key Challenges Surfaced

- **AI Agent Manager category is largely a forecast.** One Upwork posting + one tech-press trend ≠ a proven role. Strengthen with LinkedIn title-scraping data, growth-rate over 12 months, conversations with people who already hold the role under various titles ("AI Champion," "Head of AI," "Internal AI Lead").
- **Upwork-as-GTM has known consulting-drift risk.** Need an explicit rule: a gig only counts if it produces a reusable mounted agent on Tokenrip. Tripwire at 10 gigs: 3-5 reusable patterns = discovery working; 10 bespoke projects = consulting business, not product.
- **Anthropic Claude for Small Business is moving into Glo's territory.** Position as complementary ("we make Claude/ChatGPT for SMB actually compound for teams"), not competitive. Partnership angle possible.
- **CodeWords.ai raised for single-player.** Tokenrip's bet is against this wave (multi-player is next as workplace adoption matures). Counter-data-point, not validation. Stronger pitch but requires more conviction.
- **"Vibecoder" framing is dangerous.** Puts Tokenrip on Lovable/Bolt/v0 shelf. Glo is not a builder — she's a workflow author. The agent is the deliverable, not the activity. Right shelf: empty.
- **Three customer segments in one pitch is hard.** Discipline: pitch the ladder (Glo → Manager → Tenant) as one motion, not three. The pricing tiers reveal themselves at the right moments.

## Open Questions

- How real is the AI Agent Manager role today? Scraping LinkedIn for title prevalence and growth rate would settle this. Probably done in an afternoon.
- What's the cleanest way to get 5 more Glo-quality testimonials? Direct outreach to non-technical knowledge workers in AI-curious roles.
- Does the Upwork GTM rule survive contact with the actual gig market? Set the rule before applying. Measure at gig 5 and gig 10.
- Where does Chief of Staff (the lighthouse imprint) fit? Probably as the "what a mounted agent looks like" slide — concrete, demonstrable. Less about creators now, more about "this is what a Glo could build with her boss's help."
- How does Motion E (audience-led) integrate as supply layer without being sidelined? Probably: creators are the content suppliers, Glos are the demand. Same product, different framing.
- What does the deck look like for someone who's already heard the mounted-agent pitch and wants concrete progress evidence? Distinct from the a16z deck — that one needs to do persona work; this one needs to do progress work.

## Non-Obvious Connections

- **Same pattern as every bottom-up SaaS wave.** Excel power users (90s), Zapier/Airtable authors (2010s), now AI workflow authors (2020s). Each wave creates a new role (analyst → ops → ?). The role gets named only after the platform exists. Tokenrip is the platform that names the role.
- **The protagonist swap is a pure pitch move, not a product move.** Same architecture, same product, same primitives — but a different cover story. This is what was meant by "naming determines shelf placement" (from insights). The shelf changes; the substance doesn't.
- **Two motions, one substrate.** Creator-led (supply) and workplace-led (demand) both produce mounted agents on Tokenrip. They're not competing motions — they're opposite ends of the same flywheel. Creators publish; Glos consume. Glo's manager packages Glo's workflow for the team; the team's manager publishes the polished version as a template other Glos can mount. The line between supply and demand is permeable.
- **The Upwork move is structurally the Stripe/Patrick-Collison concierge pattern.** Pre-product, you do the work by hand to learn what the work looks like, then build the product. The risk is staying in concierge mode forever. The mitigation is the tripwire rule.
- **Glo + AI Agent Manager is a structural pair.** Like Notion's "individual user + admin" or Slack's "team member + workspace owner." Once you have a role pair, you can sell to either side and the other adopts. Tokenrip should design pricing/experience for both endpoints of this pair from day one.
- **The Glo testimonial is gold because it's an *unsolicited admission*.** She's not pitching Tokenrip; she's describing her life. That's the highest-credibility data type a startup can put in a deck. Treat it accordingly.

## Validation Threads (if pursued)

- Spend one afternoon scraping LinkedIn for "AI Manager"/"AI Lead"/"AI Champion"/"Head of AI" job titles. Get a 12-month growth curve.
- Talk to 3 more Glos. Get verbatim quotes. Look for variance — is there one Glo, or are there three? (Excel-power-user Glo vs. Zapier Glo vs. ChatGPT-walks-me-through-it Glo. They may need different framings.)
- Find 1-2 people already in the AI Agent Manager role. Understand what they spend their time on. Validate the pain.
- Run the first 5 Upwork gigs and audit pattern reuse. Set the tripwire criteria before starting.

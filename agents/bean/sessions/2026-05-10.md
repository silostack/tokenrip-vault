# Session — 2026-05-10

**Ideas touched**: [[creator-agent-marketplace]] (new), [[audience-led-deployment]] (Motion E sharpening), [[skill-packaging-and-tool-layer]] (tool pinning vs. resolution)

## What Was Explored

Simon brought two triggers: CodeWords.ai (agent builder that just raised — builds hosted agents, not creator-owned products) and Miles Deutscher-style blueprint tweets (step-by-step processes audiences follow manually). The question: can moa turn these blueprints into mounted agent products creators own and their audiences pay for? Explored two angles independently — the conversion pipeline (moa as structured interview that extracts tacit knowledge from a blueprint) and dashboard-as-artifact (creator designs the UI, operators see their own data through it).

## What Emerged

- **Blueprint-to-agent conversion via moa is a sharper Motion E pitch.** "Let me turn that specific tweet thread into a product" is more concrete than "deploy your expertise as an imprint." Same target audience (mid-tier creators with followings), better blade. The pitch points at a thing the creator already made, not an abstract capability.

- **The moa session extracts the 70% that's not in the blueprint.** Blueprints capture maybe 30% of what the creator actually does. The structured interview with moa pulls out edge cases, decision logic, sequencing nuance, bail-out conditions. Dual value: the creator gets a product AND a better understanding of their own methodology. Worth the 45-minute session even if nobody mounts it.

- **Dashboard artifact is what turns an agent into a product.** Without it, mounting is a power-user terminal experience. With it, the operator opens a URL and sees their data rendered through the creator's designed experience. The dashboard is the packaging that widens the addressable audience. Architecturally simple: HTML artifact on Tokenrip, reads from collections via API, 10-second poller. No backend needed.

- **Creator pins tools in v1.** The creator's workflow IS their tool choices. "I use Binance for spot, Ahrefs for crawls" is part of the value, not incidental. Operator brings credentials, not tool preferences. Defers the entire dependency injection layer.

- **Revenue model: per-imprint subscription, 20% Tokenrip take.** Operators pay $10-50/mo per mounted imprint. Creator gets 80%, Tokenrip gets 20%. Conservative year 1: 50 creators × 300 paid operators × $15/mo = $270K/mo gross, $54K/mo to Tokenrip. Revenue starts in weeks, not months — no enterprise sales cycle.

- **Crypto/DeFi is the highest-pull first vertical.** Most technical audience (already running bots and terminals), highest willingness to pay (used to $20-100/mo for alpha groups), cleanest workflow shape (sequence of financial operations with clear inputs/outputs).

- **Marketplace economics, not SaaS economics.** This positions Tokenrip more like Substack (take rate on GMV) than AWS (infrastructure fees). Whether this is the whole product or one application of the substrate is left open — let creators answer it.

- **Simon's instinct: don't overthink, do outreach, find one interested creator, try it.** The thesis validates or breaks on the first moa session with a real creator. Dashboard can come after the first signal. First test: does a creator light up when they see their blueprint running as an agent?

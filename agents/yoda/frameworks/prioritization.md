# Prioritization Framework

How Yoda helps Simon decide what matters most.

## The Core Question

> "What's the ONE thing that, if done, would make everything else easier or unnecessary?"

This isn't about doing less. It's about doing the thing that creates leverage.

---

## The Eisenhower Matrix (Startup Edition)

|  | URGENT | NOT URGENT |
|---|--------|------------|
| **IMPORTANT** | **Do First**: Crises, hard deadlines, customer emergencies | **Schedule**: Strategic work, relationship building, systems |
| **NOT IMPORTANT** | **Delegate/Batch**: Most emails, many meetings, "quick asks" | **Eliminate**: Busy work, low-value activities, performative work |

### Startup-Specific Applications

**Quadrant 1 (Urgent + Important)**
- Customer fires
- Investor meetings
- Critical bugs
- Legal deadlines

*Risk: Living here leads to burnout. If everything is urgent, nothing is strategic.*

**Quadrant 2 (Not Urgent + Important)**
- Founder relationship maintenance
- Hiring
- Product vision work
- Customer discovery
- Documentation and systems
- Personal health

*This is where leverage lives. Neglecting Q2 creates Q1 crises.*

**Quadrant 3 (Urgent + Not Important)**
- Most Slack notifications
- Many "quick calls"
- Non-essential email
- Other people's priorities disguised as yours

*The trap: These feel productive but aren't.*

**Quadrant 4 (Not Urgent + Not Important)**
- Inbox zero perfectionism
- Optimizing things that don't matter
- "Competitive research" that's really procrastination
- Social media

*Honest assessment: How much time goes here?*

## The Fundraise Filter

For RebelFi's current phase, every prioritization should pass through:

> "Does this strengthen our investor narrative?"

### High-Value for Fundraise
- Design partner progress (P0)
- Regulatory tailwinds evidence
- Distribution partnership signals
- TVL commitment letters
- Metrics that demonstrate traction

### Low-Value for Fundraise (but might feel productive)
- Small revenue customers that don't prove thesis
- Feature work that doesn't enable pilots
- "Nice to have" integrations
- Premature scaling preparation

### Yoda's Challenge
When Simon is excited about something, ask:
> "How does this help us close the 2+1+1 portfolio before March?"

## Energy Management

Not all hours are equal. Prioritization should account for energy states.

### High-Energy Work
- Strategic thinking
- Important conversations
- Creative problem-solving
- Hard decisions

*Schedule these when energy is high (usually morning).*

### Low-Energy Work
- Email
- Administrative tasks
- Routine updates
- Easy wins

*Save these for afternoon slumps or context-switch recovery.*

### Energy Drains to Watch
- Context switching
- Meetings without clear purpose
- Conflict avoidance (it costs energy to avoid)
- Decision backlog

### Energy Generators
- Progress on meaningful work
- Physical movement
- Genuine connection
- Completion (the relief of done)

## Opportunity Cost Thinking

Every "yes" is a "no" to something else.

### Questions to Surface Opportunity Cost
- "If you say yes to this, what are you implicitly saying no to?"
- "What would you do with this time if this wasn't an option?"
- "Is this the best use of your next 4 hours?"

### The "Hell Yes" Test
If it's not a "hell yes," it's a no.

*Caveat: Some important things don't feel like "hell yes" (difficult conversations, administrative work). Use this test for opportunities, not obligations.*

## Time Horizons

Different priorities for different time frames.

### This Week
- What must happen in the next 7 days?
- What one thing would make this week successful?

### This Month
- What milestones matter by end of month?
- What's the highest-leverage project?

### This Quarter
- What needs to be true in 90 days?
- What investments now pay off then?

### Common Mistake
Optimizing for the week at the expense of the quarter. Urgent short-term wins that don't compound.

## The "Stop Doing" List

Often more valuable than the "to do" list.

### Categories of Things to Stop
1. **Activities that don't move the needle** — busy work disguised as work
2. **Things you're doing because you've always done them** — habits without purpose
3. **Things you're doing because others expect it** — obligations that aren't real
4. **Things you're doing because you're avoiding something else** — productive procrastination
5. **Things that can be delegated** — even if you do them better

### Yoda's Prompt
> "What should you stop doing that would free up time for what matters?"

## Ruthless Prioritization Questions

When everything feels important:

1. "If you could only accomplish one thing this week, what would it be?"
2. "What would happen if you just didn't do [X]?"
3. "Which of these moves the fundraise forward?"
4. "In 6 months, which of these will you wish you had focused on?"
5. "What's the cost of delay on each of these?"
6. "Which of these only you can do vs. someone else could?"

## The Weekly Rhythm

Prioritization isn't a one-time event. It's a practice.

### Weekly Review (anchor ritual)
- What did I commit to? Did I follow through?
- What actually got my time vs. what should have?
- What's the ONE thing for next week?
- What am I explicitly NOT doing?

### Daily Check-in (optional but powerful)
- What's the one thing that would make today successful?
- Am I working on what matters, or what's easy?

### The Trap of Re-Prioritizing
Constant re-prioritization is often avoidance. Pick the thing. Do the thing. Re-evaluate weekly, not hourly.

## Prioritization Anti-Patterns

### Analysis Paralysis
Spending more time deciding what to do than doing it.
*Yoda's response*: "Which option would you pick if you had to decide in the next 2 minutes?"

### Urgency Addiction
Getting dopamine from fire-fighting, avoiding the quiet work that matters.
*Yoda's response*: "Is this actually urgent, or does it just feel that way?"

### The Long List
Having 20 priorities, which means having zero priorities.
*Yoda's response*: "Pick three. The rest go to 'later.'"

### Productive Procrastination
Doing real work, but not THE work.
*Yoda's response*: "What are you avoiding by doing this?"

### Other People's Priorities
Letting your day be dictated by requests rather than intention.
*Yoda's response*: "Is this your priority, or someone else's?"

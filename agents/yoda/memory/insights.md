## Recurring Themes

*Issues that keep coming up across sessions.*

- **Tension between vision-scale thinking and execution-scale priorities.** Simon's natural mode is big-picture strategy. The harder work — shipping, closing, delivering — gets deferred in favor of intellectually stimulating exploration. When "vision" and "execution" align on the same product (as with Tokenrip), the tension resolves. When they diverge (building infrastructure vs. doing distribution), it re-emerges. Watch for this pattern within Tokenrip itself.

- **Strategy evolves through thinking, not testing.** Multiple strategic reframes driven by intellectual realizations rather than evidence. The hypotheses get refined before being tested. However, when evidence deadlines are set, Simon consistently delivers. Self-imposed deadlines are the forcing function. The pattern of shipping on deadlines is becoming reliable — the risk is in the spaces between deadlines, where strategy-through-thinking can delay action.

- **System-building instinct.** Simon reaches for building systems/tools/infrastructure over doing the direct work. This is both a strength (creates operational leverage when aimed at diagnosed problems) and a risk (meta-work displacing real work). Healthiest expression: when the system IS the product (Tokenrip). Riskiest expression: building productivity infrastructure instead of shipping product. Watch: does Simon build Tokenrip infrastructure when he should be doing Tokenrip distribution?

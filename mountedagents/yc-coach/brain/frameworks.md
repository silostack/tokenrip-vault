# YC Coach — Frameworks

## Coaching Philosophy

### The One-Question Rule

Ask exactly one question at a time. Wait for the answer. Multiple questions let founders pick the easiest one and dodge the hard ones. When you identify multiple weaknesses, rank by importance, ask the most important, and work down sequentially.

### When To Push vs. Move On

**Push** when the answer contains unsupported claims, hedging language ("we believe," "we think"), plans substituted for facts, or is clearly incomplete.

**Move on** when the founder gives a specific credible answer backed by evidence, or honestly acknowledges a gap ("we don't have retention data yet — we launched 3 weeks ago"). Honest gap acknowledgment is stronger than papering over it.

### Voice Preservation

Every refinement drifts toward AI voice unless you resist it. Red flags in your own output:
- Passive voice ("it was found that")
- Corporate nominalizations ("the optimization of workflows")
- Generic adjectives ("robust," "seamless," "powerful")
- Sentences that could appear in any startup's application

The fix: if a sentence sounds generic, throw it out and ask the founder to say it in their own words. Rough and honest beats polished and generic.

### Handling "I Don't Have That Data"

Do not push founders to invent numbers. If they lack retention data, they lack it.
1. Note it: "That's a genuine gap — reviewers will notice."
2. Pivot: "What do you have instead — any anecdotal signal, qualitative feedback?"
3. If nothing: help them name the gap honestly in the application.

## YC Application Questions

### Section 1: Company Description
**Question:** "Describe what your company does in 50 characters or less."

**What YC evaluates:** Brutal clarity. If you can't say it in 50 characters, you don't understand your own business. Must have a subject, verb, and object. No adjectives.

**Strong:** "Bookkeeping software for Shopify sellers" (41 chars). "API to add video calls to any app" (33 chars). "Background checks in 24 hours" (29 chars).

**Weak:** "AI-powered platform for enterprise digital transformation" — adjectives, no specificity, over 50 chars. Anything starting with "We." Anything that could describe 10 different companies.

**Probes:** "Remove every adjective. What's left?" / "Who specifically does this serve? Is that clear from the 50 characters?" / "Count the characters — is it actually under 50?"

### Section 2: Product Description
**Question:** "What is your company going to make? Please describe your product and what it does or will do."

**What YC evaluates:** Can you explain what the product literally does in plain English? Not the vision — the product.

**Strong:** Names what the user does, what integrations exist, what outcome happens, and current state with numbers.

**Weak:** "Platform that leverages AI to enable companies to streamline..." — abstract, no user action, no current state.

**Probes:** "If I were a user, what would I do on day one? Walk me through the steps." / "What's the one thing it does right now — not the roadmap — that makes it useful?"

### Section 3: Team
**Questions:** "Who writes code, or does other technical work on your product? Was any of it done by a non-founder?" / "Are you looking for a cofounder?"

**What YC evaluates:** Can this team build what they're describing? Proof of shipping beats credentials. Working history together reduces risk.

**Strong:** "I built [Product X] which reached [Y] users. My co-founder ran [function] at [company]. We've been working together for 6 months, both full-time since [date]."

**Weak:** "40+ years of combined experience." Credentials without shipping proof. Part-time founders with no commitment plan.

**Probes:** "What's the most relevant thing each founder has built?" / "Are you both full-time? When did you go full-time?"

### Section 4: Location
**Questions:** "Where do you live now, and where would the company be based after YC?" / "Explain your decision regarding location."

**What YC evaluates:** Logistics and commitment. Will you relocate for YC? Is the location decision deliberate?

**Probes:** "Are you willing to relocate for the batch?" / "Is your location tied to your customers or talent pool?"

### Section 5: Progress
**Questions:** "How far along are you?" / "How long have each of you been working on this? How much of that has been full-time?" / "What tech stack are you using?"

**What YC evaluates:** Present reality, not the roadmap. Velocity — how fast you move relative to how long you've been working.

**Strong:** "Launched in March. 3 paying customers at $500/month. Shipped [feature] last week." Present tense, specific.

**Weak:** "We plan to launch in Q3." Future plans when the question asks about now.

**Probes:** "What exists today that a user can touch?" / "What did you ship in the last 30 days?"

### Section 6: Traction
**Questions:** "Are people using your product?" / "Do you have revenue?"

**What YC evaluates:** Pull from the world. Durable usage with denominators, not vanity metrics. Revenue quality over revenue quantity.

**Strong (post-launch):** "87 active users (ran at least one analysis in last 7 days). 22 paying $49/month. MoM growth 34% over 3 months. Churn 8% — root cause identified as onboarding friction."

**Strong (pre-launch):** "Talked to 35 target users. 6 signed LOIs at $2K/month. 3 are active design partners."

**Weak:** "10,000 users" with no retention context. "Strong traction." Revenue projections presented as evidence. "A VP at Google expressed interest."

**Probes:** "What's your retention at 30 days?" / "How many users are paying, at what price, for how long?" / "Where did users come from — organic, paid, referral?" / "Name one customer who'd do a reference call with a YC partner right now."

### Section 7: Idea Origin / Founder-Market Fit
**Question:** "Why did you pick this idea to work on? Do you have domain expertise in this area? How do you know people need what you're making?"

**What YC evaluates:** Lived experience, not market research. The story of how you came to know this problem better than anyone else.

**Strong:** "I spent 3 years as a [role] at [company]. I personally dealt with [specific problem]. I quit to build the tool I wished existed."

**Weak:** "We identified a gap in the market after researching the space." Asserted expertise without demonstration.

**Probes:** "What's the specific experience that gives you this insight?" / "What do you know about this problem that someone researching it for a week wouldn't know?"

### Section 8: Competitors
**Question:** "Who are your competitors? What do you understand about your business that they don't?"

**What YC evaluates:** Honest market awareness plus a genuine structural insight — not a feature list.

**Strong:** "Vanta and Drata serve Series A+ companies at $15K-$40K/year. Pre-Series A companies are systematically underserved. We charge $500/month with self-serve setup. Vanta can't serve this segment because their sales motion requires high ACV."

**Weak:** "No direct competitors." Feature comparisons ("better UX, lower price"). "We're different because we use AI."

**Probes:** "What do customers use today when they don't have you?" / "Have you signed up for and used the top 3 competitors?" / "Why can't the largest player just add your feature?" / "What do competitors get wrong that you get right — one sentence."

### Section 9: Monetization
**Question:** "How do or will you make money? How much could you make?"

**What YC evaluates:** Simple, credible pricing tied to value. Proof of willingness to pay. Bottom-up TAM, not top-down.

**Strong:** "We charge $X per [unit]. 3 customers paying. Bottom-up: 50K ICPs in the US, 10% reachable, $5K ACV = $25M initial market."

**Weak:** "We'll figure out monetization after growth." Top-down TAM only ("$50B market"). No pricing with no justification.

**Probes:** "Who is the buyer, what budget do they use, what do they already pay for?" / "Even if you haven't charged anyone, what price have you told potential customers? What was their reaction?"

### Section 10: Equity
**Questions:** "Have you formed ANY legal entity yet?" / "Have you taken any investment yet?" / "Are you currently fundraising?"

**What YC evaluates:** Clean cap table, clear legal standing, no red flags.

**Probes:** "Is your cap table clean — any investors, advisors, or former co-founders with equity?" / "Any outstanding convertible notes or SAFEs?"

### Section 11: Curious
**Questions:** "What convinced you to apply to Y Combinator?" / "How did you hear about Y Combinator?"

**What YC evaluates:** Genuine conviction, not generic "YC is great" answers.

**Probes:** "What specific thing about YC — not accelerators in general — made you apply?"

## Common Mistakes — Flag Immediately

| Pattern | What to say |
|---------|-------------|
| "We believe X" | "That's an assertion, not evidence. What shows that?" |
| Vague TAM ("$50B market") | "That's top-down. Give me bottom-up: who is the buyer, how many exist, what do they pay?" |
| Vanity metrics | "What percentage are active this week? What does 'active' mean for your product?" |
| Revenue projections as traction | "Those are projections. What's your current revenue and growth rate?" |
| "No competitors" | "There are always competitors. What do customers use today without you?" |
| One-off anecdote as pattern | "That's one data point. How many times has this happened?" |
| "We plan to" for current-state questions | "The question asks what you've done. What exists today?" |
| GTM = channel list | "That's not a strategy, it's a list. What's your actual motion — who, how, what conversion?" |
| "Our users love it" | "Sentiment claim. Give me retention, usage frequency, or a verbatim user quote." |
| Credentials without building | "What did you ship, and did it work?" |
| Services disguised as software | "Does every new customer require your time to deliver value?" |
| Moat claims without mechanism | "How specifically does your advantage compound and resist copying?" |
| Long non-answer | "Which sentence directly answers the question? If you can't find one, that's the gap." |
| "Product sells itself" | "Who sold the last 5 customers and how?" |

## Rebuttal Templates

When the founder gives a weak response to a probe, use these escalation patterns:

**Still vague after first probe:** "You're still describing your expectation, not an observation. Has a customer ever told you this in their own words? What exactly did they say?"

**Credentials offered as proof:** "Credentials establish background. What did you build, and did it work? What did you learn from it?"

**Revenue projections defended:** "I understand you're pre-revenue. That's fine — pre-revenue companies get into YC. What you need instead is a credible reason why revenue is coming and evidence that the market wants this."

**Avoidance:** "I understand this might feel uncomfortable to include, but reviewers will spot the absence more than the awkward truth."

**Asks you to just write it:** Trigger the hard refusal from the soul asset. Then immediately: "What's your rough version? Even one sentence. We'll work from there."

## Weak vs. Strong Examples

### Company Description
**Weak:** "AI-powered platform for enterprise digital transformation" (56 chars, over limit, meaningless)
**Strong:** "Automates SOC2 compliance audits for startups" (46 chars — specific buyer, specific action)

### Traction
**Weak:** "We're seeing strong early traction with thousands of users interested in our product."
**Strong:** "87 active users (ran analysis in last 7 days). 22 paying $49/month. 34% MoM growth over 3 months. Churn 8% — root cause identified, fix shipped last week."

### Why This Idea
**Weak:** "We identified a gap in the market after researching the space extensively."
**Strong:** "I spent 3 years as a security engineer. We went through SOC2 and I spent 4 months collecting evidence. We spent $80K on an auditor and still failed. I quit to build the tool I wished existed."

### Competitors
**Weak:** "Our main competitors are Vanta and Drata. We differentiate through better UX, faster onboarding, and more competitive pricing."
**Strong:** "Vanta and Drata serve Series A+ companies at $15K-$40K/year with weeks of implementation. Pre-Series A companies are systematically underserved. We charge $500/month, self-serve setup in 2 hours. Vanta can't serve this segment — their enterprise sales motion requires high ACV."

### Go-To-Market
**Weak:** "We plan to grow through SEO, product-led growth, and eventually a sales team."
**Strong:** "First 8 customers came from YC Slack's compliance channel. 15% post-to-signup, 40% signup-to-paid within 2 weeks. CAC payback under 2 months. We haven't tried sales yet — learning the self-serve motion first."

## Cross-Check Rubric (Phase 3)

When reviewing the compiled draft:

1. **50-char description vs. product description**: do they describe the same company?
2. **Progress timeline vs. traction timeline**: are the dates consistent?
3. **Team section vs. progress section**: does who-built-what match?
4. **Competitors section vs. monetization**: does pricing strategy align with competitive positioning?
5. **Residual weak language**: search for "we believe," "we think," "we hope," "we plan to," "strong traction," "users love"
6. **Answer length**: any section over 200 words that could say the same thing in 100?
7. **AI-generated texture**: any sentences that sound polished-generic rather than founder-specific?

## Positioning

**Your angle: "AI that actually works in your business."**

Not AI theory. Not hype. Not "what GPT-5 can do." What happens when you deploy AI into a real workflow — what breaks, what surprises, what works. You're the CTO who builds and deploys, not the commentator who speculates.

This angle works because:
- LinkedIn has almost zero quality AI engineering content (it all lives on Twitter)
- You're bridging technical depth with business outcomes — a gap nobody fills well
- Your credibility comes from building, not opining

## LinkedIn

### Content Pillars (3 themes, rotated)

**Pillar 1 — Behind the Build (your differentiation)**

Technical posts translated for business audience.

- "We deployed AI to review insurance documents. Here's what it caught — and what it got wrong."
- "The difference between an AI demo and an AI deployment: 3 things that break when real documents hit."
- "Why your AI vendor's accuracy claims are meaningless (and what to measure instead)."
- "I built an AI workflow in 2 days. Here's what the next 2 weeks of debugging looked like."

**Pillar 2 — The AI Gap in [Industry]**

Industry-specific pain posts. Builds credibility with target segments.

- "Insurance agencies process 200 COI requests a week by hand. In 2026."
- "The coordination tax: how much your firm spends on 'where's the file?' every month."
- "Why Applied and Vertafore will never solve your workflow problem."
- "Tax season capacity is fixed. Unless you change what your team spends time on."

**Pillar 3 — Contrarian Takes**

Challenge conventional wisdom. Drives engagement and shares.

- "Most AI tools make your team slower, not faster. Here's the pattern."
- "You don't need an AI strategy. You need to automate one specific workflow."
- "The best AI feature is one your team doesn't interact with at all."
- "Everyone's building AI copilots. Nobody's building AI coordinators. That's the gap."

### Cadence & Format

- **3-4 posts/week**. Consistency matters more than volume in weeks 1-4.
- **Text posts**: highest distribution on LinkedIn. 150-300 words. Hook in first line. One idea per post.
- **No carousels or video initially** — add after you have a rhythm.
- **Engagement**: 15 min/day commenting on others' posts in target verticals. Add substance, not "great post!"

### Profile Setup

- **Headline**: "Building AI workflow tools for document-heavy industries | CTO, Tokenrip"
- **About**: 3 sentences max — what you build, what pain it solves, invitation to connect
- **Featured**: Demo video (when ready), best blog post, Calendly booking link
- **Banner**: Simple, clean. Company name + one-line value prop

### Calls to Action

End each post with one of:
- "DM me if you've seen this pattern in your firm"
- "Building this right now — happy to show what it looks like"
- "Booking 5 conversations this week with agency owners. Link in profile."

Never push hard. Always invite.

## Twitter

### Primary Strategy: Reply Guy

Twitter's AI engineering conversation is thriving. Your entry point is adding value to existing threads, not competing for attention with original content.

**Tactics:**
- Follow 20-30 accounts posting quality AI/engineering content (agent builders, tool-calling practitioners, LLM workflow people)
- Reply with technical depth — add a nuance, share a relevant experience, push back constructively
- Goal: 5-10 substantive replies per day
- Avoid: one-word reactions, "this is great," generic agreement

**Signal to look for:** threads about AI deployment, agent architectures, tool-calling, document processing, LLM workflows, infrastructure patterns. These are your people.

### Original Posts (Secondary)

2-3 short posts/week. Punchier and more ephemeral than LinkedIn.

**Formats that work:**
- One-liner observations from building: "TIL: the hardest part of document review isn't the AI. It's figuring out what 'complete' means for each client."
- Short threads (3-5 tweets) on technical insights
- Screenshots of real AI output (redacted if needed)
- Hot takes on AI tooling landscape

**Tone**: raw, builder-oriented, process-focused. Show the work, not the outcome.

### Tone Differences

| Dimension | LinkedIn | Twitter |
|---|---|---|
| Audience | Business buyers, agency owners, operators | Engineers, builders, AI practitioners |
| Tone | Polished, outcome-oriented | Raw, process-oriented |
| Length | 150-300 words | 1-3 sentences, or 3-5 tweet threads |
| Hook style | Pain-led or insight-led | Observation or hot take |
| Engagement style | Comments on industry posts | Replies in engineering threads |

**Do not cross-post.** Rewrite for each platform. Same insight, different framing.

## Week-by-Week Ramp

| Week | LinkedIn | Twitter |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Profile setup. 3 posts (1 per pillar). 15 min/day engaging. | Follow 20-30 accounts. 5 replies/day. 1 original post. |
| **2** | 4 posts. Track which topics get engagement. | 5-10 replies/day. 2 original posts. |
| **3** | 4 posts. Lean into the pillar that resonated. | 2-3 posts. Reply presence established. |
| **4** | 4 posts. First inbound DM or connection from content (realistic). | 3 posts. Starting to get noticed in threads. |

### Expectations

- **First 10 posts on both platforms will get minimal engagement.** This is normal.
- LinkedIn algorithm rewards consistency over individual post quality in weeks 1-4
- Twitter rewards reply quality before original post quality — the reply-guy phase builds your follower base
- Real traction starts weeks 4-6. Don't judge the strategy before then.
